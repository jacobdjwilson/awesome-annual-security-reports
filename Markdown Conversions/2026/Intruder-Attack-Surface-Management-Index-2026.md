## Introduction
The 2026 Attack Surface Management (ASM) Index provides a comprehensive analysis of the evolving threat landscape. As organizations continue to expand their digital footprints through cloud adoption and remote work, the complexity of managing an external attack surface has increased exponentially. This report outlines the current state of vulnerability management and the efficacy of proactive security measures.

## Executive Summary
In 2026, the primary challenge for security teams remains the "visibility gap." Our research indicates that over 60% of organizations are unaware of at least one-third of their internet-facing assets. This report highlights the critical need for continuous monitoring and automated discovery tools to mitigate risks associated with shadow IT and unpatched vulnerabilities.

## Methodology
The data presented in this index was compiled through a combination of:
- Automated scanning of public-facing infrastructure.
- Analysis of Common Vulnerabilities and Exposures (CVE) databases.
- Surveys conducted with Chief Information Security Officers (CISOs) across various industries.

## Key Findings
1. **Proliferation of Shadow IT**: Unmanaged assets continue to be the leading entry point for attackers.
2. **Mean Time to Remediate (MTTR)**: While detection speeds have improved, remediation times remain stagnant due to resource constraints.
3. **Cloud Misconfigurations**: Misconfigured S3 buckets and open ports remain the most common findings in external audits.

## Recommendations
- **Implement Continuous Discovery**: Move away from point-in-time assessments toward real-time asset inventory.
- **Prioritize Risk-Based Patching**: Focus remediation efforts on vulnerabilities that are actively being exploited in the wild[^1].
- **Automate Compliance**: Utilize Infrastructure as Code (IaC) scanning to prevent misconfigurations before deployment.
