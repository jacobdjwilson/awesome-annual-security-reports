## Executive Summary

The 2026 Global Scam Intelligence Report provides a comprehensive analysis of the current threat landscape regarding digital fraud, social engineering, and automated scam campaigns. As artificial intelligence continues to lower the barrier to entry for malicious actors, the sophistication and volume of scams have reached unprecedented levels. This report outlines the primary vectors of attack, the demographic impact, and the necessary defensive postures for organizations and individuals alike.

## The Evolving Landscape of Digital Scams

In 2026, the convergence of generative AI and deepfake technology has fundamentally altered the nature of digital deception. Scammers are no longer reliant on manual outreach; instead, they utilize autonomous agents to conduct personalized, high-scale phishing campaigns across multiple platforms, including messaging apps, social media, and traditional email.

### AI-Driven Social Engineering
The use of real-time voice cloning and synthetic video generation has enabled a surge in "CEO fraud" and "family emergency" scams. These attacks leverage high-fidelity impersonation to bypass traditional authentication methods.

### Automated Scam Infrastructure
Modern scam operations now function as "Scam-as-a-Service" (SaaS) models, where infrastructure, target lists, and evasion tools are rented out to less technically proficient criminals.

## Key Findings and Statistics

- **Platform Shift**: While email remains a primary vector, 35% of successful scams now originate from encrypted messaging platforms.
