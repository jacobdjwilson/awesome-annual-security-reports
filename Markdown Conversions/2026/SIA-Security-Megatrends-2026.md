## Introduction
The 2026 Security Megatrends report serves as the annual vision for the security industry, providing insights to inspire, inform, and prepare stakeholders for the opportunities and challenges ahead.

These trends are now common concerns that all business leaders must manage:

- Cybersecurity
- Global Tensions

- Changing Economic Conditions
- Supply Chain Assurance
- Sustainability
- Workforce Development

## Executive Takeaways from SNG
![Collage of quotes from industry leaders regarding data, AI, and customer-first business models]

## 1. Software Eats the World. Will AI Eat Software?
The industry has shifted from hardware-dominant to software-dominant. While cloud-based SaaS solutions continue to grow, artificial intelligence is now introducing a new layer of disruption. Agentic AI may replicate functionality once hard-coded into applications, fundamentally changing how software is designed and utilized.

## 2. The Security Hardware Layer is Reinvented
Hardware is evolving beyond simple sensing to become intelligent and integrated. By embedding software and AI-lite analytics, hardware becomes an active contributor to a "system of systems." The future favors hardware that supports digital twins and feeds clean, normalized data into platforms.

## 3. Security Solutions Lose Their Boundaries
Security is expanding into a holistic ecosystem integrating building systems, IT infrastructure, and operational technologies. Buyers now seek multi-use technologies that deliver operational insights alongside security, such as predictive maintenance and improved situational awareness.

## 4. The Value Chain Replaces the Channel Model
The traditional linear channel model (manufacturer to distributor to integrator to user) is being replaced by a Value Chain. This model centers on customer outcomes, where stakeholders "build-with" one another to solve specific enterprise challenges rather than simply pushing products through a pipeline.

## 5. Posthuman Automation of Security
The industry is entering an era where agentic AI redefines operations. Rather than replacing humans, AI is expected to augment capabilities by automating routine tasks like monitoring, alarm ranking, and reporting, allowing professionals to focus on high-priority risk management.

## 6. End-to-End Solutions and One-Logo Approaches
Vendors are expanding beyond single-product categories to offer comprehensive, end-to-end solutions. This "one-logo" approach appeals to buyers seeking to reduce complexity and standardize their security infrastructure, mirroring IT strategies.

## 7. The Unification of the Security Experience Layer
Unification is moving beyond simple integration to a consolidated data layer. By aggregating video, access, and sensor data into a single architecture, organizations can surface actionable insights and enable predictive analytics, democratizing advanced capabilities for smaller organizations.

## 8. Security Operations Centers (SOCs) and Monitoring Will Be Disrupted and Automated
- **SOC Transformation**: The "showcase" SOC with massive video walls is being replaced by virtualized, decentralized teams supported by AI.
- **Alarm Monitoring**: AI is predicted to largely replace human monitoring within seven years, shifting the industry from passive detection to active deterrence.

## 9. Correcting the Systemic Undervaluation of Security
The industry is pivoting toward a "Return on Security" strategy. By moving away from the "cost center" mindset and adjusting pricing to reflect the true value of innovation, the industry can better justify higher compensation to attract top-tier talent.

## 10. Security Technology Refresh Cycles Accelerate
The "run-to-fail" mindset is being replaced by accelerated refresh cycles driven by cybersecurity demands, AI adoption, and IT oversight. Outdated operational technology is now viewed as a liability, necessitating more frequent updates to maintain network integrity and functional capability.
