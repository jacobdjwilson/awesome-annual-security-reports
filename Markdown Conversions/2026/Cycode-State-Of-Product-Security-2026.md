## Lior’s Foreword: The Great Convergence: Secure Software by Default in the AI Era

The AI revolution has forced teams to take a new approach to product security. That’s because AI innovation is outpacing adoption, and adoption is outpacing security. More code is being generated faster than ever thanks to AI co-pilots, and AI tools are being adopted and integrated into workflows without careful governance. 

> “Security that extends across the entire product lifecycle (from the code developers generate, to the supply chains that support it, to the way applications are built, delivered, and maintained) is the only way to manage risk at the scale that today's software ecosystems demand.” — Lior Levy, CEO, Cycode

Real convergence means purpose-built specialization inside a single platform. It means cutting through the noise, reducing false positives, and giving security teams the clarity they need to focus on what matters most.

## Vibe Coding and the Rise of the 10X Developer

### The Vibe Coding Phenomenon
When we say "vibe coding," we're talking about the intuitive, rapid generation of code with an AI code assistant. Developers can sketch an idea in plain language, let an AI model build the code, and move on. 

### The 10X Developer Dilemma
For experienced engineers, AI tools act as force multipliers. But less experienced developers may rely on AI to write the majority of their code, lacking the experience to recognize insecure coding practices or business logic flaws.

## Executive Summary

Our latest research shows that 100% of organizations today have AI-generated code in their codebase, and 97% are actively using or piloting AI coding assistants. AI adoption has already happened. But as AI transforms how software is built, it’s also breaking how it’s secured. The result is a runaway train that legacy application security tools and strategies cannot contain.

---

## Research Methodology

Cycode commissioned an independent, vendor-agnostic survey of 400 security leaders across the UK and US in October 2025.

- **Respondents:** 400 (40% CISO, 40% AppSec Team, 20% DevSecOps Team)
- **Company Size:** 50% (1k-5k employees), 50% (5k+ employees)

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **AI-Generated Code Is Security’s Biggest Blind Spot**: 97% of organizations use AI coding assistants; only 19% have full visibility.
2. **Shadow AI Expands the Attack Surface**: Unapproved AI tools bypass procurement, creating hidden dependencies.
3. **Governance and Security Oversight Are Falling Behind**: 52% lack centralized governance for AI.
4. **Product Security Is Emerging as the Linchpin**: 99% of organizations now have product security coverage.
5. **Convergence Is the Path Forward**: 97% of organizations plan to consolidate their AppSec stack within 12 months.

---

## Insight #1: AI-Generated Code is Security’s Biggest Blind Spot

![Chart showing percentage of codebase generated by AI: 1-25% (18%), 26-50% (52%), 51-75% (28%), 76-100% (2%)]

AI agents aren’t vetted employees; they are opaque systems that introduce new risks. Securing this code has become the top priority for product security teams.

---

## Insight #2: Shadow AI and Tool Sprawl Expand the Attack Surface

Shadow AI—the use of unapproved tools—creates a new class of untracked dependencies. Security leaders cite AI tool usage (54%) and software supply chain risks (51%) as their top blind spots.

---

## Insight #3: Product Security Is the Unsung Hero of the AI Era

Product security has evolved into a central discipline. 
- **Top 5 Product Security KPIs:** 1. Fewer Vulnerabilities in Production, 2. Developer Adoption, 3. Faster Time-to-Fix, 4. Compliance Readiness, 5. Reduced Tool Sprawl.

---

## Insight #4: AI Visibility is Lagging Behind

![Chart showing 81% of organizations lack complete visibility into AI usage]

Only 19% of organizations report complete visibility of where and how AI is being used. 65% of respondents report an overall increase in security risk since adopting AI.

---

## Insight #5: Governance and Compliance Are the Missing Links in AI Security

More than half (52%) of organizations still lack any formal or centralized governance framework for managing AI adoption. Forward-looking organizations are expanding traditional SBOMs to include **AI Bills of Materials (AIBOMs)**.

---

## Insight #6: Productivity and Time-to-Market Validate AI’s Promise

77% of organizations report higher developer productivity, and 72% point to faster time-to-market. However, this speed comes at the cost of security, as governance has not kept pace.

---

## Insight #7: AI Adoption Creates a Push-and-Pull Between IT, Engineering, and Security

There is a tug-of-war between the drive for innovation and the need for security. Security teams are often forced into a reactive role, chasing shadow AI and playing catch-up on oversight.

---

## Insight #8: Security Budgets are Higher Than Ever

39% of organizations expect to increase AI-related AppSec spending by 50% or more in the next year. 

**Top 3 Priorities:**
1. Securing AI-generated code (50%)
2. Improving developer productivity without sacrificing security (45%)
3. Defending against adversarial AI-powered attacks (44%)

---

## Insight #9: AI Has Changed What Security Leaders Expect From Vendors

Security leaders are looking for:
- AI-driven prioritization of vulnerabilities (66%)
- Automated detection of vulnerabilities in AI-generated code (66%)
- Secure-by-default AI code generation guidance (60%)
- AI-assisted remediation suggestions and fixes (57%)

---

## Insight #10: More Tools, More Chaos. Convergence Is the Answer

97% of organizations plan to consolidate their application security tools in the next 12 months. 

![Chart showing consolidation plans: 50% Partial, 47% Full, 3% No]

Real convergence means a purpose-built platform where AST (Application Security Testing), SSCS (Software Supply Chain Security), and ASPM (Application Security Posture Management) work in concert.
