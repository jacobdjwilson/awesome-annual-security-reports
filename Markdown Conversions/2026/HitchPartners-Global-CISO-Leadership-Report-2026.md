## Introduction & Methodology

Presented by Hitch Partners, the 2026 Global Organization Report analyzes the evolving landscape of security leadership. Now in its ninth annual edition, the report provides critical insight into compensation trends, reporting structures, and the expanding responsibilities of security executives in 2025.

This year's analysis examines both Chief Information Security Officers (CISOs) and NextGen security leaders. While CISOs continue to shape security strategy and communicate risk at the executive level, NextGen security leaders who operate just below the CISO are playing an increasingly pivotal role in executing strategy, owning key security programs, and driving operational excellence.

### Methodology
This report is based on survey responses from more than 625+ Information Security executives across North America (U.S. and Canada) and select international markets. Responses were collected between Q4 2025 and Q1 2026.

### Scope & Definitions
- **"CISO" Definition**: The most senior security leader accountable for an organization's information security strategy, program execution, and risk management.
- **"NextGen" Definition**: The security leadership layer directly reporting to the CISO—typically the top 3-5 security leaders responsible for executing the security program across specialized domains.

## Executive Summary

The 2026 Global CISO Report reveals fundamental shifts in compensation, reporting structures, and governance.

- **$128K**: Public company vs. private company CISO compensation gap.
- **30-32%**: CISOs reporting to CTO/Engineering.
- **2%**: Optimized AI governance.
- **36%**: Private company CISOs without liability protection.
- **43%**: CISOs reporting third-party risk as #1 priority.
- **+6% YoY**: NextGen compensation growth outpacing CISO.

## Section 01: Compensation Analysis

Public companies maintain significant compensation advantages across all components, with equity driving the largest differentials.

![Chart showing Public vs Private CISO Total Compensation: Public $814K vs Private $686K]

### Industry Compensation Leaders
Consumer Software & Internet leads at $928K total compensation, driven by the highest equity packages ($458K avg). Media/Entertainment ($882K) and Logistics/Transportation ($864K) follow closely.

### Geographic Insights
Compensation differences by location remain significant, with a 2.4x spread between top-paying markets such as Seattle and lower-cost metros like Kansas City.

### Employment Offer Bonus Trends
Public companies offer signing bonuses 79% more frequently and at 80% higher amounts than private counterparts.

## Section 02: Reporting Structure Evolution

Security leadership has fundamentally realigned toward technical execution, with CTO and senior engineering leaders now representing the dominant reporting structure.

- **CTO/Engineering Line**: Represents 30-32% of CISO reporting relationships (+5% YoY growth in private companies).
- **CIO Reporting**: Remains significant at 22% (private) and 34% (public).
- **Company Size**: At companies under 500 employees, 32% of CISOs report directly to the CEO. This collapses to just 3% at enterprises exceeding 10,000 employees.

---

## Section 03: CISO Liability Protection

Security leaders face significant personal risk with inadequate executive and personal liability coverage.

- **Executive Liability**: 36% of private and 20% of public CISOs lack D&O/indemnification coverage.
- **Personal Liability**: ~74% of CISOs lack personal liability insurance coverage, regardless of company structure.

---

## Section 04: Team Size Dynamics

Security team scaling follows a non-linear trajectory that peaks at upper mid-market scale before federation begins.

- **Peak Complexity**: Teams peak at 5K-10K employees (243 personnel).
- **Federation Effect**: Beyond 10K employees, teams contract 47% as organizations federate security responsibilities across platform teams and business units.
- **Tenure**: CISOs at sub-500 employee companies average just 28 months tenure.

---

## Section 05: CISO Functional Responsibilities

The data reveals three distinct clusters:
1. **Universal operational functions**: Incident Response, Cloud Security, SecOps (88-93%).
2. **Converging risk functions**: Privacy, GRC, TPRM (82-85%).
3. **Fragmented emerging functions**: AI Ethics, Post-Quantum Cryptography, Fraud (18-30%).

---

## Section 06: AI Governance and Risk Management

Organizations face a critical gap between AI adoption velocity and security preparedness.

- **Leadership Vacuum**: Only 6% of private and 13% of public companies have dedicated AI security leaders.
- **Maturity**: 73% lack mature AI governance (52% developing/inconsistent, 21% initial/ad hoc).
- **Top Concerns**: Shadow AI and accountability definition are the top two challenges. 75% of CISOs cite data exposure/privacy breaches as the top AI risk.
- **Frameworks**: NIST AI RMF dominates with 57-67% adoption.

---

## Section 07: Threat Landscape 2026

- **Top Priorities**:
    1. Third-party risk (43%)
    2. AI-enhanced attacks (22%)
    3. Cloud misconfigurations (15%)
- **Budget Justification**: 69% justify security budgets via business impact, marking a shift from "cost of doing business" to "enabler of business outcomes."

---

## Section 08: NextGen Security Leaders

NextGen compensation growth is outpacing CISO increases, signaling execution-layer talent scarcity.

- **Compensation**: Execution capability now commands a higher premium than strategic oversight.
- **Span of Control**: At enterprise scale (10K+), NextGen security leaders manage an average of 72 direct reports.

---

## Section 09: International CISO Landscape

International CISOs earn $469K total compensation, which is 32% below the North American average of $750K.

- **Regulatory Drivers**: International CISOs show significantly higher ownership of TPRM (85%) and Privacy (73%), driven by GDPR, NIS2, and DORA.
- **Team Scaling**: Higher enterprise team sizes suggest less federation in international markets compared to North America.

---

## Section 10: Strategic Imperatives for 2026

1. **Close the Compensation Gap**: The $128K public-private delta creates retention risk.
2. **Navigate CTO-Line Reporting**: Security must demonstrate technical enablement value.
3. **Mature AI Governance**: 95%+ of organizations lack mature AI governance frameworks.
4. **Secure Liability Protection**: 36% of private company CISOs operate without executive liability protection.
5. **Manage Third-Party Risk**: The #1 security priority for 43% of respondents.
6. **Develop NextGen Pipeline**: Execution-layer talent scarcity requires proactive development.
