## Executive Summary: Record Capital Expands Opportunity to Address More Sources of Volatility

Record-breaking capital and a benign hurricane season set the stage for fierce competition at January 1, exacerbated by growing appetite from third party capital providers for insurance risk. Capitalizing on a buyers’ market, insurers were able to secure significant decreases in pricing and improved terms overall – creating new opportunities to reinvest savings to address customers’ unmet needs and drive profitable growth with new solutions.

### Reinsurer capital reaches new high
Global reinsurer capital reached a new high of $760 billion at the end of September 30, 2025, driven largely by reinsurers’ retained earnings and record levels of third-party capital. Despite total industry insured losses from natural catastrophes exceeding $100 billion for the sixth consecutive year, reinsurers are set to produce yet another year of strong results in 2025. The reinsurance sector reported an average annualized return on equity of 16 percent for the first nine months of 2025, well in excess of the average cost of equity.

### Buyers explore options to reinvest premium savings
With excess capacity in the market and stable demand, insurers achieved favorable outcomes at the January 1 property renewals. Competition was intense and widespread as reinsurers sought to grow and demonstrate their relevance, while third-party capital markets continue to expand and broaden their appetite. As a result, insurers were able to achieve significant decreases in pricing and improved terms at renewal.

### Widening pool of capital
With each year, the relevance of alternative sources of capital continues to grow. Third-party capital reached a new high of $124 billion at the end of the third quarter, an increase of $9 billion relative to the end of 2024. The catastrophe bond market ended the year at an all-time high with more than $24 billion issued across 74 sponsors and $59 billion in catastrophe bonds outstanding.

### Turning resilience into profitable growth
With a widening pool of capital seeking growth opportunities, the reinsurance industry has a significant opportunity to address unmet client needs, large pools of underinsured and uninsured risk, and emerging exposures around the world. From climate to casualty, and AI to an aging population, these shifts are creating new insurable assets, new concentrations of risk and new product needs, essentially opening potential new markets.

## Global Reinsurer Capital: Strong Earnings and New Inflows Push Capacity to New Highs

Aon estimates that global reinsurer capital rose to a new high of $760 billion at September 30, 2025, an increase of $45 billion relative to the end of 2024. Growth was driven by retained earnings, unrealized gains on bonds taken directly to equity and new inflows to sidecars and the catastrophe bond market.

## Traditional Reinsurer Results: A Third Year of Strong Performance

Most reinsurers will produce a third strong result in succession in 2025. Although the year began badly with the California wildfires, ceded loss activity was otherwise relatively benign. Consequently, most reinsurers remained well within their annual major loss budgets and strong underlying margins were reflected in an average combined ratio of 91.0 percent at the nine-month stage.

## Spotlight: Scaling (Re)Insurance Capacity for Global Data Center Expansion

Driven by global growth in AI, data and cloud services, the rapid expansion of data centers presents one of the most significant (re)insurance growth opportunities in decades. Between 2026 and 2030, cumulative global insurance premiums associated with data centers are estimated to be $134 billion.

### Structural Drivers of Capacity Demand
- **Financing-driven insurance requirements**: Third-party financing is driving greater insurance demand for mega projects.
- **Rising values and limit needs**: Large, multi-building data center campuses can require insurance limits of $10 billion or more.
- **Speed to market and market share**: The “speed-to-capacity” imperative requires insurers and reinsurers to respond quickly with scalable capital.

## Demand-Supply Market Dynamics

### Property: Excess Capacity Reinforces Buyers’ Market for Property Cat
Building on the positive momentum at renewals in 2025 and a benign U.S. hurricane season, reinsurance buyers achieved favorable results at the January 1 property renewals. Capital was abundant with supply significantly outstripping demand.

### Facultative: Flexible Options to Support Profitable Growth
An increasingly favorable facultative reinsurance market offers insurers an expanding range of flexible and complementary solutions to de-risk portfolios and support growth.

### Casualty: Increasing Capacity and Growth Opportunities
Conditions in the casualty reinsurance market at 1/1 were relatively favorable as increased capacity and reinsurer appetite helped fuel growing competition for international casualty and stability for U.S. placements.

## Specialty: By Line of Business

- **Aviation Reinsurance**: A stable outcome. Reinsurers continue to be most comfortable offering coverage on an excess of loss basis.
- **Marine, Energy and Terror**: Benign losses spark favorable terms. Excess of loss pricing reduced by low to mid double-digit figures.
- **Trade Credit, Structured Credit and Political Risk, and Surety**: Increased capacity and positive claims trends bolster stable conditions.

---

## Life and Health Spotlight: Product Innovation to Close the Protection Gap

- **U.S.**: Life insurers are increasingly collaborating with private equity firms, leading to a meaningful revamp of traditional investment approaches.
- **Latin America**: Pricing for life and personal accident insurance has remained broadly stable.
- **EMEA**: Overall capacity, demand and risk appetite remain stable, but pressure persists in certain lines, particularly long-term disability and medical expenses.
- **APAC**: The Asia Pacific life and health reinsurance market in 2025 is marked by steady expansion, ample capacity and a more deliberate use of reinsurance as a balance sheet and risk management tool.
