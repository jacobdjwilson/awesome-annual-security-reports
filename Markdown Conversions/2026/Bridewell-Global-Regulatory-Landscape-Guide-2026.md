## What’s New: UK and Europe

### UK: Key Movements to Track
- The European Commission has reaffirmed that the UK provides an adequate level of data protection. As a result, personal data can continue to flow from the EEA to the UK under safeguards considered broadly equivalent to those in the EU. This decision is valid until 27 December 2031.
- Following Royal Assent, the UK’s Data Use and Access Act (DUAA) is being rolled out in phases across 2025–26 - with most provisions commencing two to six months post-Assent and certain elements extending up to twelve months into 2026.
- The Cyber Security and Resilience Bill will bring medium and large IT/cyber security providers into scope to strengthen resilience for essential services (e.g., health, water, transport, energy) (Implementation timing expected as 1–2 years).
- The Crime and Policing Bill includes an amendment aimed at stopping the use of AI image and video generators to create prohibited content. It signals that regulators may place clearer legal duties on organisations across the AI content “supply chain,” with implementation likely in the next 1–2 years.

### EU: Implementation and National Transposition Focus
- **Cyber Resilience Act** – implementing rule for “important/critical” products (effective 1 Dec 2025): Defines which digital-element products must undergo stricter conformity assessment - relevant for device/software manufacturers. Planning launches in 2026.
- **NIS2 national measures**:
    - Germany enacted its NIS2 implementation law for federal administration (effective 6 Dec 2025), reinforcing essential information-security management.
    - Portugal transposed NIS2 via Decree-Law 125/2025, applying to essential/important entities across public and private sectors (with core national-security carve-outs).
- **EU AI Act national enablement**: Hungary’s AI Act (2 Aug 2026) sets up an institutional and enforcement framework to operate the EU AI Act domestically.
- **Non-EU: New Data Privacy Regime**: Bosnia and Herzegovina adopted its local Data Protection law in October 2025 which closely aligns with General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).

## What’s New: USA

The US remains a state-by-state patchwork, with fast-moving developments in privacy, child/teen online protections, breach response, and AI accountability. There is a shift toward operational controls: risk assessments, privacy-by-default for minors, transparency obligations for AI/data practices, and stronger enforcement.

January 2026 marked the effective date for several privacy measures in different states:
- California introduces new risk assessment obligations, under-16 data treated as sensitive (right-to-limit), a 30-day breach notification deadline, and stronger penalties for unregistered data brokers.
- Indiana’s Consumer Data Protection Act also takes effect, setting a baseline privacy regime for in-scope organisations, with consumer rights and transparency obligations broadly aligned to the Virginia-style model.

Later in the year, California’s and Colorado’s AI measures start to take effect. From 30 June 2026, Colorado’s AI Act introduces a risk-based framework for high-risk AI systems to reduce algorithmic discrimination. From 2 August 2026, California’s AI Transparency Act begins a phased rollout of provenance/disclosure expectations for AI-generated content, with staged milestones continuing into 2027.

Across other states, three themes stand out:
- **Child/teen safety** drives Vermont’s Age-Appropriate Design Code Act (effective 1 January 2027) and Michigan’s HB 5357 (~18–24 months), both pushing high-privacy defaults and restrictions on profiling, sales, targeted ads and dark patterns for minors.
- **Sensitive data regulation** is tightening via Pennsylvania’s HB 1530 (~1–2 years), targeting direct-to-consumer genetic testing with enhanced consent, security, and deletion/sample-destruction expectations.
- **AI transparency and security** are accelerating in New York (algorithmic pricing disclosure and AI “companion” rules already effective, plus the proposed RAISE Act), alongside phased cyber security regulation amendments; Florida’s proposed AI Bill of Rights and data-centre measures point to growing links between AI governance and wider technology infrastructure controls.

## What’s New: Global

Outside the EU/UK and USA, regulation is accelerating, with many jurisdictions moving toward enforceable privacy, tighter cross-border transfer controls, stronger cyber security oversight, and early but fast-maturing AI governance.

January 2026 marks the effective date for several major Asia-Pacific measures: Vietnam’s Personal Data Protection Law takes effect on 1 January 2026; China’s Cybersecurity Law amendments also take effect on 1 January 2026; and China’s Measures for Authentication of Personal Information Exported Abroad begin the same day.

March 2026 brings a further AI milestone, with Vietnam’s AI Law effective 1 March 2026.

Other themes stand out across different jurisdictions:
- **Cross-border transfer compliance** is becoming more structured, with Chile’s Model Contractual Clauses (valid until December 2026) and India’s DPDPA Rules phasing in through May 2027.
- **Comprehensive privacy regimes** and enforcement readiness are expanding (e.g., Gambia’s DP law and proposed reforms in Bangladesh and Colombia). A longer-horizon datapoint is Paraguay’s DP act listed for November 2027.
- **Cybersecurity and “trustworthy AI” controls** are tightening, including Singapore’s expanded cybersecurity oversight, Canada’s proposed critical cyber systems framework, and practical AI security/assessment guidance from South Korea and Australia.
