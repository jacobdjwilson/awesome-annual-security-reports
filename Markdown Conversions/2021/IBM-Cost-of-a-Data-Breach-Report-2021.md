## Executive summary

Now in its 17th year, the Cost of a Data Breach Report has become one of the leading benchmark reports in the cybersecurity industry. This report offers IT, risk management and security leaders a lens into dozens of factors that can increase or help mitigate the rising cost of data breaches.

With research conducted independently by the Ponemon Institute, this report – sponsored, analyzed, and published by IBM Security – studied 537 real breaches across 17 countries and regions and 17 different industries.

### Key findings
- **10% Increase in average total cost of a breach, 2020-2021**: Data breach costs rose from $3.86 million to $4.24 million, the highest average total cost in the history of this report.
- **$1.07m Cost difference where remote work was a factor in causing the breach**: The average cost was $1.07 million higher in breaches where remote work was a factor.
- **11 Consecutive years healthcare had the highest industry cost of a breach**: Healthcare data breach costs increased from $7.13 million in 2020 to $9.23 million in 2021, a 29.5% increase.

![Infographic showing key findings including lost business share, per record cost of PII, and share of breaches caused by compromised credentials]

- **38% Lost business share of total breach costs**: Lost business represented the largest share of breach costs, at an average total cost of $1.59M.
- **$180 Per record cost of personally identifiable information**: Customer PII was the costliest record type.
- **20% Share of breaches initially caused by compromised credentials**: Compromised credentials was the most common initial attack vector.

![Infographic showing average days to identify/contain, cost multiplier of mega breaches, and zero trust impact]

- **287 Average number of days to identify and contain a data breach**: Seven days longer than in the previous report.
- **100x Cost multiplier of > 50 million records vs. average breach**: Average cost of a mega breach was $401 million for breaches between 50 million and 65 million records.
- **$1.76m Cost difference in breaches where mature zero trust was deployed vs. no zero trust**.

![Infographic showing security AI/automation impact, hybrid cloud costs, and compliance failure costs]

- **80% Cost difference where security AI and automation was fully deployed vs. not deployed**.
- **$3.61m Average cost of a breach in hybrid cloud environments**.
- **$2.30m Cost difference for breaches with high vs. low level of compliance failures**.

## How we calculate the cost of a data breach

To calculate the average cost of a data breach, this research excludes very small and very large breaches. Data breaches examined in the 2021 study ranged in size between 2,000 and 101,000 compromised records.

This research uses an accounting method called activity-based costing. Four process-related activities drive expenditures:
1. **Detection and escalation**: Forensic and investigative activities, assessment and audit services, communications to executives and boards.
2. **Notification**: Emails, letters, outbound calls, communication with regulators, engagement of outside experts.
3. **Post breach response**: Help desk, inbound communications, credit monitoring, identity protection, legal expenditures, regulatory fines.
4. **Lost business**: Business disruption, revenue losses from system downtime, cost of lost customers, reputation losses.
