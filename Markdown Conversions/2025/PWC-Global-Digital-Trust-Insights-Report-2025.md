## Bridging the gaps to cyber resilience: The C-suite playbook

Findings from the 2025 Global Digital Trust Insights survey of 4,042 business and tech executives from across 77 countries revealed significant gaps companies must bridge before achieving cyber resilience.

### Key Findings
- **2%**: Only 2% have implemented cyber resilience actions across their organisation in all areas surveyed.
- **50%**: Under 50% of CISOs are involved to a large extent in key business activities.
- **13%**: Point gap in confidence between CISO/CSOs and CEOs regarding compliance with AI and resilience regulations.

## Navigating cyber threats: Establishing a shared vision for preparedness

While the cybersecurity landscape continues to evolve, organisations are struggling with increasingly volatile and unpredictable threats. 

### Unprepared for the most concerning threats
The top four cyber threats found most concerning — cloud-related threats, hack-and-leak operations, third-party breaches and attacks on connected products — are the same ones security executives feel least prepared to address.

### The strategic divide: Business and tech priorities
- **66%** of tech executives rank cyber as the highest risk for mitigation, compared to 48% of business executives.
- **42%** of executives rank cloud-related threats as their most concerning cyber threat.
- Global average data breach cost exceeds $3 million (estimated at $3.32 million).

> "Don’t stop short on your journey for cybersecurity and resilience. Criminals and nation-state actors are becoming expert at finding unprotected seams: weak identity and access controls, unpatched devices and security misconfigurations." — Rob Joyce, Cyber, Risk & Regulatory Senior Fellow, PwC US.

## GenAI and emerging tech: Balancing opportunity and risk

### An evolving attack surface
- **67%** of security executives say that GenAI has increased their attack surface over the last year.
- **78%** have increased their investment in GenAI over the last 12 months.
- **72%** have increased their risk management investment in AI governance.

### Leveraging GenAI for cyber defence
Executives are using GenAI for threat detection and response, threat intelligence, and malware/phishing detection. However, obstacles include difficulty incorporating with existing systems (39%), lack of trust (39%), and inadequate internal controls (38%).

## A highly regulated cyber world: Are companies really ready?

### Cyber regulations are driving positive change
- **96%** report that cybersecurity regulations have spurred them to increase their cyber investment in the last 12 months.
- **78%** believe that regulations have helped to challenge, improve or increase their cybersecurity posture.

### Confidence gap
There is a 13-point gap in confidence between CISO/CSOs and CEOs regarding compliance with AI and resilience regulations. CISOs are less optimistic than CEOs, likely due to their proximity to operational difficulties and resource constraints.

## Unlocking the potential of cyber risk quantification: What’s holding organisations back?

- **15%**: Only 15% are measuring the financial impact of cyber risks to a significant extent.
- **87%**: Say allocating resources to areas of highest risk is of high importance.
- **44%**: Say data issues are a top challenge faced when quantifying the financial impact of cyber risk.

---

## Investing in resilience, building trust

- **77%** expect their cyber budget to increase next year.
- **48%** of business executives prioritise data protection and data trust as the top cyber investment.
- **34%** of tech executives prioritise cloud security as the top cyber investment.

> "The threat landscape is increasingly unpredictable, as we’re seeing multi-vector threats to physical and digital environments. We’re investing resources toward integrated response and recovery capabilities to enhance physical security and cybersecurity." — Dr. Georg Stamatelopoulos, CEO of EnBW AG.

## Is your cyber strategy and leadership driving real resilience?

### Partial implementation isn’t enough
Only 2% of organisations have implemented all 12 resilience actions surveyed. Key areas lagging include:
- Establishing a resilience team (34%).
- Developing a cyber recovery playbook for IT-loss scenarios (35%).
- Mapping technology dependencies (31%).

### Elevating the CISO
Fewer than half of executives report that their CISOs are largely involved in strategic planning for cyber investments, board reporting, and overseeing tech deployments.

> "It’s the CISO’s job to contextualise and connect the threats that exist to the vulnerabilities within the organisation. That means educating people on the threats the enterprise is prepared to deal with and those it’s not ready for." — David Bruyea, CISO at Moneris.

## About this report
The 2025 Global Digital Trust Insights is a survey of 4,042 business and technology executives conducted between May and July 2024. Respondents represent 77 countries across various industries, including industrials, tech, media, telecom, financial services, and energy. The survey is conducted by PwC Research.
