## About This Survey and the Team8 CISO Village

Team8’s CISO Village is a private, exclusive community of security executives from the world’s most influential companies. Each year, we host a five-day summit where CISOs engage in off-the-record conversations, share best practices, and shape the future of cybersecurity innovation.

The insights in this report are drawn from our annual CISO Village Survey, completed by over 110 security leaders during the 2025 Summit. With respondents from GuideWire, Respol, Elastic, Centene, and other industry leaders, this survey offers a rare window into how the world’s top security teams are thinking, reacting, and building.

---

## 1. Budgets & Buying Behavior

Cybersecurity budgets remain on a strong upward trajectory. This year, 52% reported a budget increase – a signal of continued investment following an exceptionally high-growth year in 2024, where 70% saw gains.

That spike, in our view, represented a post-COVID correction; What we’re seeing in 2025 is not a pullback, but rather a return to steady, strategic growth driven by long-term planning, operational pressure, and the rising complexity of the threat landscape.

![Chart showing budget changes: 52% Increased, 37% Did not change, 11% Decreased]

### Best-of-Breed Makes a Comeback
60% of CISOs prefer best-of-breed tools over best-of-suite platforms, potentially marking a turning point in the “platformization” wave. While platforms once promised simplicity and scale, many CISOs are finding that breadth often comes at the expense of depth.

## 2. The AI Arms’ Race

AI is no longer on the horizon, it’s in the kill chain. We are witnessing a true arms race between attackers and defenders.

### 2.1. AI-Powered Attacks
25% of CISOs reported experiencing an AI-generated attack within the past 12 months. The most visible examples today remain AI-generated social engineering attacks: deepfakes, voice cloning, and real-time impersonation.

### 2.2. Securing Enterprise AI Adoption
In 2025, AI risk has become the defining security challenge for CISOs. Two specific issues rise to the top: securing AI agents (37%) and governing employee use of AI tools (36%).

### 2.2.1. AI Agents
AI agents are autonomous software entities that can perceive, reason, and act across enterprise systems. 
- 67% of enterprises are deploying agents in 2025.
- 23% are planning to follow in 2026.
- 9% have no plans to introduce agents.

![Chart showing development methods: 67% In-house, 59% SaaS, 58% No-code, 49% Productizing]

### 2.2.2. Securing Employee AI Usage
Shadow AI refers to tools that enter the organization through individual teams or employees without going through IT or security review. 
- 43.4% of organizations restrict usage to approved tools.
- 25.7% allow use with policies but no monitoring.
- 22.1% allow and monitor usage.

### 2.2.3. AI for Security
CISOs expect AI to replace human labor in high-volume, process-heavy tasks:
- SOC Analyst: 77%
- Third-party risk management: 27%
- Pentesting: 27%
- Provisioning and access reviews: 26%
- Design review threat modeling: 22%

## 3. 2025 Top CISO Pain Points

1. **Securing AI Agents**: 39%
2. **Governing AI usage**: 36%
3. **Data leakage prevention (DLP)**: 33%
4. **AI-Augmented SOC**: 28%
5. **TPRM**: 23%
6. **Infrastructure vulnerability management**: 24%
7. **Secure products by design**: 21%
8. **Human risk management**: 21%
9. **Securing AI-generated code**: 20%
