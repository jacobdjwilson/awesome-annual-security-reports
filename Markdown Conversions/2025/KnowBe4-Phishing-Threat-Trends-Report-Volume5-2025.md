## Keeping Up With The Changes
AI-powered polymorphic phishing is changing the threat landscape for good. In 2024, at least one polymorphic feature was present in 76.4% of all phishing attacks. These campaigns consist of almost identical emails that differ by small details, making them difficult for traditional Secure Email Gateways (SEGs) to detect.

![Graph showing the percentage of polymorphic phishing emails throughout 2024, ranging from 42.2% in January to 74.3% in December.]

### AI Threw Fuel On The Fire
In 2024, 73.8% of all analyzed phishing emails exhibited some use of AI; this increased to 90.9% when inspecting emails with polymorphic elements. Attackers are increasingly using randomized characters, symbols, and invisible characters to bypass Natural Language Processing (NLP) and Natural Language Understanding (NLU) detection systems.

## Ransomware On The Rise (Again)
We are seeing a surge in ransomware attacks with more sophisticated tactics. Between September 15, 2024, and February 15, 2025, there was a 22.6% increase in ransomware payloads in phishing attacks.

### Advanced Obfuscation Techniques
- **HTML Smuggling:** Used to mask malicious payloads from file scanning.
- **AI-Generated Obfuscation:** Random text added to payloads to confuse security scanners.
- **Obfuscated Malicious URLs:** URLs are split, reversed, and encoded using Base64 to evade blocklists.

## You’re Hired!
Engineering roles are a primary target for cybercriminals due to their high level of system access. Attackers often disguise malware as coding challenges.

- **The "Kyle" Case:** KnowBe4 identified a fake employee who used an AI-manipulated headshot and a stolen social security number to attempt to install malware on a corporate laptop.
- **Targeting:** 64% of job-application-related phishing emails target engineering roles.
- **Shared Inboxes:** 52% of these attacks are sent to shared mailboxes to increase the pool of potential victims.

## What’s Getting Through Your Traditional Email Security Defenses?
In 2024, there was a 47.3% increase in attacks evading detection by Microsoft and SEGs. Attackers are increasingly using:
- **URL Redirects:** Using multiple "hops" to hide the final destination.
- **Hijacked Legitimate Domains:** Leveraging trusted platforms like Google, DocuSign, and SharePoint.
- **Technical Obfuscation:** Using image-based emails, invisible characters, and homoglyph Unicode characters.

## By The Numbers: How You’re Being Phished In 2025
- **17.3%** increase in phishing emails.
- **57.9%** of attacks sent from compromised accounts.
- **82.6%** of phishing emails utilized AI.
- **1058** average characters per phishing email.
- **Top three words:** Urgent, Review, Sign.

## It’s Time To Change Or Be Left Behind
We are in a new age of phishing threats characterized by unprecedented scale. Organizations must move beyond signature-based detection and embrace Human Risk Management (HRM). By combining advanced technical controls with employee training, organizations can better defend against the evolving threat landscape.
