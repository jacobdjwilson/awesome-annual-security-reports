## Connected Kids: From AI Friends to Cyberbullying

In an age where children are more connected than ever, this report shines a light on kids’ real digital behaviors and the challenges they face. As kids grow up on the digital playground—where AI friendships, cyberbullying, and screen-time battles are the new norm—parents are navigating an increasingly complex and emotional landscape.

## Global Results: AI and Cyberbullying

Globally, 1 in 8 (13%) parents say their children have been victims of cyberbullying and 36% of parents say they have noticed their child using AI for companionship, conversation, or emotional support.

In the U.S., 1 in 4 parents (24%) say their children have been victims of cyberbullying and 28% say they have noticed their child using AI for companionship, conversation, or emotional support.

![Chart showing 36% global total and 28% US total for AI usage, and 13% global total and 24% US total for cyberbullying victims.]

## The Faces of Cyberbullying

Of the 13% of parents who say their child has been a victim of cyberbullying, the top perpetrators were:

- A classmate or peer: 43%
- An older adult or teen: 39%
- A former friend or ex-friend group: 30%
- A stranger online: 27%
- Someone they met through online gaming: 27%
- A sibling or family member: 15%

81% of parents said they are comfortable discussing cyberbullying with their child.

## Platforms for Cyberbullying

Cyberbullying is no longer confined to traditional social media feeds. Kids today report experiencing it across a wide range of platforms:

- Facebook: 43%
- Instagram: 39%
- TikTok: 31%
- Online Gaming: 27%
- Text: 25%
- YouTube: 23%

## AI Usage and Parental Sentiment

**Top AI Tools Used by Children (Global Total):**
- ChatGPT: 48%
- Google Gemini: 30%
- AI tools in school: 28%
- Microsoft Copilot: 19%
- Snapchat My AI: 15%
- Character.AI: 13%

**Parents' Opinions of AI on children’s learning and creativity (Global Total):**
- AI is beneficial: 43%
- AI is not beneficial: 30%
- Unsure: 29%

57% of parents say they have discussed AI’s risks with their kids (e.g., deepfakes, misinformation, etc.).

## Glimmer of Hope: Guidance and Literacy

- 57% of parents have spoken with their children many times about how to stay safe online.

## Screen Time Management

- 82% of parents actively manage their kid’s screen time.
- 3.83 hours: Average amount of time that parents think their kids spend on screens per day (globally).
- 55% of parents are concerned about their kids' screen time habits.

**Despite that:**
- 29% of parents say their child found a way to get around the screen time limits or parental controls and told their parents.
- 23% of parents say their child got around the controls and thought they got away with it.

## 5 Tips to Help Keep Kids Safer

1. **Start the Conversation Early**: Don’t wait until there’s a problem—talk to your children about online behavior, safety, and kindness.
2. **Use Parental Control Tools Thoughtfully**: Take advantage of built-in device settings and tools like Norton Family to set boundaries.
3. **Teach Kids to Recognize Red Flags**: Help children understand the signs of cyberbullying, scams, AI manipulation, or predatory behavior.
4. **Model Healthy Tech Use**: Set a positive example by putting devices down during family time.
5. **Stay Involved, Stay Curious**: Regularly check in with your child about what they’re doing online.

## Survey Method

The study was conducted online in 11 countries worldwide by Dynata on behalf of Gen from April 24th to May 9th, 2025, among 11,006 adults ages 18 and older. Data are weighted where necessary by age, gender, and region, to be nationally representative. Within this report, mentions of parents refer to parents with children younger than 18 years old.
