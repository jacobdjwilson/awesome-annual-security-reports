## Overview
Cloud architectures are evolving faster than most security teams can adapt. As hybrid, multi cloud, edge, and SaaS adoption accelerates, organizations are contending with fragmented environments, inconsistent controls, and expanding attack surfaces. Detection is delayed, tooling is overloaded, and many defenses remain outdated—all while adversaries automate, adapt, and scale their own capabilities. The result is a growing mismatch between how the cloud is being used and how it’s being secured.

To better understand how security leaders are responding to these pressures, Cybersecurity Insiders set out to examine the real-world strategies, priorities, and constraints shaping cloud defense today. Through a comprehensive survey of over 900 CISOs, cybersecurity professionals, and IT decision-makers conducted in early 2025, this report captures the current state of cloud security from the CISO’s perspective — including what’s working, what’s breaking down, and where organizations are investing next.

### Key Findings Include:
- **Cloud adoption is accelerating across every architectural layer**: 62% of organizations expanded cloud-edge technologies (like SASE), 57% expanded hybrid cloud, and 51% adopted multi cloud.
- **Cloud-related breaches are rising fast**: 65% of organizations experienced a cloud-related incident in the past year; only 9% detected it within the first hour, and 62% took more than 24 hours to remediate it.
- **Detection tools fail to surface threats**: Only 35% of organizations detected incidents via security monitoring tools; most incidents are discovered by end users, third parties, or during audits.
- **Security operations are under strain**: 71% of organizations use over 10 cloud security tools, and 45% receive over 500 daily alerts.
- **AI is rising as a top security priority**: 68% of organizations say AI adoption is a priority, yet only 25% are confident in their ability to defend against machine-driven attacks.
- **Application-layer security remains dangerously outdated**: 61% still rely on signature-based WAF detection as their primary defense.

## 01 Cloud Complexity Outpaces Security
Cloud infrastructure is becoming more distributed, dynamic, and difficult to defend. As hybrid and multi-cloud adoption grows, traditional security models built for centralized environments are breaking down.

![Chart showing growth in cloud-edge, hybrid, and multi-cloud adoption]

### Cloud Strategies Reshape Risk
This divergence signals a critical inflection point: security teams no longer secure “the cloud” as a centralized perimeter, but a constantly shifting matrix of platforms, providers, and traffic flows.

### Defending Data, Identities, and Unseen Threats
![Chart of biggest cybersecurity challenges: Hybrid/Multi-cloud security (38%), Threat visibility (40%), Remote workforce (36%), High-value assets (43%), Incident response (35%)]

### Lateral Movement Remains a Hidden Risk
In cybersecurity, visibility is everything. According to our survey, only 17% of organizations have full visibility into lateral traffic within their cloud environments. The rest either have partial (54%) or no visibility at all (29%).

### Why Security Strategies Fall Short
The most widely cited barrier is the pace of technological change (54%), followed by a shortage of skilled personnel (49%) and poor integration between platforms (40%).

## 02 Rising Security Incidents
Cloud-related breaches are rising sharply, and most organizations are detecting them late, remediating them slowly, and discovering them reactively.

- 65% of organizations reported a cloud-related incident in the past 12 months.
- 38% take more than 24 hours to detect an incident.
- Only 35% discovered incidents through their security tools.

### Detection Remains Largely Reactive
![Chart showing discovery methods: Security tools (35%), Affected users (23%), Incident response (12%), External notification (10%), Routine audit (9%), Other (11%)]

### Delayed Detection Widens the Blast Radius
Only 9% of organizations detected a cloud-related incident within the first hour. 38% of organizations say it takes more than 24 hours to detect an incident.

### Slow Remediation Extends Risk Exposure
62% of organizations take more than 24 hours to remediate an incident.

## 03 The Hidden Cost of Cloud Complexity
Cloud security spending is rising — not just in dollars, but in time, complexity, and burnout.

- 71% of organizations use more than 10 tools to secure their cloud environments.
- 89% say the cost of managing cloud security across multiple providers is moderately to extremely significant.

### Tool Sprawl Weakens Cloud Defense
![Chart showing number of security tools: 1-10 (24%), 11-25 (36%), 26-50 (19%), 51-100 (12%), +100 (4%)]

### Tool Fatigue Leads to Alert Fatigue
45% of security teams receive 500 or more alerts per day.

---

## 04 AI Security Modernization
AI is rapidly becoming a core security priority — but defenders still feel outpaced by AI-powered threats.

- 68% of organizations rank AI adoption as a cybersecurity priority.
- Only 25% feel confident defending against AI-driven attacks.
- 61% of organizations primarily use signature-based WAF detection.

### AI Roadblocks
Top AI adoption barriers include lack of skilled personnel (62%), integration friction (56%), and limited model explainability (32%).

## Turning Insight into Action: Cloud Security Priorities for 2025
1. **Architect security for a decentralized cloud**: Shift to a distributed security approach.
2. **Network security is more crucial than ever**: Embed real-time inspection and lateral movement detection.
3. **Break the alert fatigue cycle**: Apply a hybrid mesh layer to automate the ecosystem.
4. **Integrate before you consolidate**: Prioritize solutions that share intelligence.
5. **Use AI to accelerate defense**: Look for solutions designed to manage the unstructured nature of GenAI.
6. **Modernize app-layer defenses**: Shift to WAF strategies that use AI/ML to detect behavioral anomalies.

## Methodology and Demographics
This cloud security survey was conducted in early 2025 and gathered responses from 937 cybersecurity professionals. The survey maintains a 95% confidence level with a ±3.2% margin of error.

- **Career Levels**: Specialist (27%), Manager (23%), Director (19%), C-Suite (9%), Consultant (6%), Owner (4%), Other (12%).
- **Company Size**: Ranging from 10 to over 10,000 employees.
- **Industries**: Financial Services (21%), Technology (18%), Healthcare (14%), Government (9%), and others.
