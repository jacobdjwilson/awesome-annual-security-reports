## Most Phished Topics in 2025

- **January: Promotions, Benefits and Remuneration** (33% of phishing emails featured HR-related topics).
- **February: Valentine’s Day** (34.8% increase in Valentine's related traffic vs. February 2024).
- **March: Missed Messages** (March 17th was the most-phished day so far; 18.4% increase compared to 2024; 245% increase in malicious SVG attachments).
- **April: Tax** (22.9% increase in tax-related attacks vs. April 2024; Top 3 terms: Tax, IRS, and Payment).
- **May: Meta Impersonation** (Attackers exploited AppSheet to send emails impersonating Meta).
- **June: Microsoft Mailbox Limit** (15.9% of impersonation attacks referenced Microsoft mailbox storage limits; 3x more social engineering attacks).
- **July: Company Impersonation** (23.4% of phishing attacks impersonated the recipient’s company).
- **August: The US Open** (Cybercriminals impersonated event vendors including Rolex, IBM, and IHG; high proportion exploited direct send vulnerability in Microsoft 365).
- **September: Start of University Academic Year** (6.3% increase in attacks sent from compromised student accounts).
- **October: Hijacking Legitimate Payment Platforms** (10x increase in phishing attacks sent using platforms such as PayPal, Strike, QuickBooks, Venmo, and Cashapp).
- **November: Multi-Factor Authentication** (Growing trend using fraudulent MFA emails to make credential-harvesting sites seem authentic).
- **December: Holiday Celebrations** (Exploitation of religious and secular celebrations).

## Caught in the Spider’s Web
### What Happens After Scattered Spider Breaches a Retail Giant?

In April 2025, the criminal gang **Scattered Spider** breached UK retail giants Marks and Spencer (M&S) and Co-Op. Known for identity-based attacks, the group has been linked to high-profile breaches including MGM Resorts, Caesars Entertainment, and the 2024 Snowflake breach.

#### How the Scattered Spider Weaves Its Web
Scattered Spider uses social engineering to gain network access. Tactics include:
- Email and SMS-based credential harvesting.
- SIM swapping fraud.
- MFA bombing.
- Voice phishing (vishing).
- Impersonating technology providers (e.g., Okta).
- Use of the adversary-in-the-middle (AiTM) phishing kit “Evilginx”.

## Hello…? Hello…?
### Who Answers When You Call a Cybercriminal?

Between January 1st and September 30th, 2025, 5.5% of phishing emails used a phone number as the sole payload—a 449% increase compared to 2024.

- **AI vs. Human**: 77.3% of vishing attacks used an AI voice; 22.7% used a human.
- **Motivations**: 68.7% of calls were financially motivated (changing bank details, fraudulent refunds, or financial transfers).

## A Daily Deception
### When Do Cybercriminals Send Phishing Emails?

Globally, phishing attacks peak during working hours, Monday through Friday. Attacks are most frequent from mid-morning until mid-afternoon.

## Is This Legit?
### The Continued Hijacking of Legitimate Platforms

The use of legitimate platforms (SharePoint, DocuSign, PayPal, etc.) to send phishing emails has increased by 66.9% YTD compared to 2024. 

- **DMARC Compliance**: 100% of analyzed attacks passed DMARC.
- **Platform Trends**: In 2025, Intuit QuickBooks, Google AppSheet, and Zoom were frequently abused.

## What’s Getting Through Secure Email Gateways?
### How Attacks Are Engineered to Evade Traditional Defenses

Since the end of 2023, there has been a 38.3% increase in phishing emails evading Secure Email Gateways (SEGs) and a 44.2% increase in bypassing Microsoft’s native detection. 

- **Polymorphic Techniques**: 32.4% of attacks now use randomized subject lines.
- **Obfuscation**: Increased by 13.6% this year, with HTML smuggling being the most common technique.

## By the Numbers: A Quick Round-up of Phishing Stats From 2025

- **Volume**: 15.2% increase in phishing email volume compared to the previous six months.
- **AI Usage**: 5.1% increase in attacks using AI-generated text or automation.
- **Compromised Accounts**: 59.1% of phishing attacks were sent from compromised accounts (a 34.9% increase vs. 2024).
- **Targeted Roles**: CEO, CFO, CPO, VP of Finance, and COO.
- **New Employee Vulnerability**: New employees receive a phishing email on average 3.5 weeks after starting.
- **Generational Targets**: Millennials are the most phished generation, followed by Gen X.

## A New Age for Human Risk Management

Email security can no longer exist in isolation. Organizations must adopt a holistic Human Risk Management (HRM) ecosystem that combines threat intelligence, behavioral analytics, and continuous coaching to protect against sophisticated, multi-channel attacks.
