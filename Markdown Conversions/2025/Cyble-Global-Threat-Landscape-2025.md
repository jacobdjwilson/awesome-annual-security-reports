## Executive Summary
The first half of 2025 saw an unprecedented 54% increase in ransomware attacks compared to the same period in 2024, totaling 3,201 reported incidents. February was the most active month, with 848 attacks, largely driven by the aggressive campaigns of the CL0P ransomware group.

### Key Highlights
- **North America remained the most targeted region**, accounting for 65% of all attacks, with the United States alone experiencing 1,814 incidents (57% of the global total).
- **The Construction and Professional Services sectors** were the most attacked globally.
- **Three ransomware operators—CL0P, Akira, and Qilin—were behind 34% of all attacks**, emphasizing the growing dominance of fewer, more capable threat groups.
- **Supply Chain Threats on the Rise**: A 30% rise in software supply chain attacks was observed, with April alone recording 31 incidents. Most targeted were IT, telecom, and tech service providers, exposing downstream organizations in finance, government, and healthcare.
- **Emerging Ransomware Groups**: Groups like Dire Wolf, DATACARRY, Silent Team, and Gunra have surfaced with new leak sites, targeting victims across Asia, Europe, and the Americas.
- **Hacktivism Evolves into Industrial Threat**: Hacktivist campaigns in 2025 showed a dramatic increase in sophistication and scale. Russia-linked groups Z-Pentest, Sector 16, and Dark Engine launched coordinated attacks on industrial control systems (ICS) in energy, transportation, and utilities sectors.

## CL0P, Akira, Qilin Ransomware Operators Top Contributors

### Cl0P
The Cl0P ransomware group, active since at least 2019, is a financially motivated threat actor known for high-impact, large-scale cyber extortion campaigns. Their hallmark is the use of double extortion: encrypting victims’ data and leaking stolen information on their dedicated leak site, “CL0P LEAKS.”

Cl0P is known for exploiting zero-day vulnerabilities in enterprise file transfer software, such as MOVEit (CVE-2023-34362) and GoAnywhere MFT (CVE-2023-0669). Unlike many RaaS operations, Cl0P is more centralized, controlling the entire attack lifecycle.

### Akira
Akira has primarily targeted the North American region, with a focus on the U.S. In Europe, Germany was the most attacked country, specifically targeting the manufacturing and professional services sectors.

### Qilin
Qilin operates as a Ransomware-as-a-Service (RaaS) provider. They were notably active in the U.S., Europe, and Asia, with a significant April 2025 attack on the UK’s NHS, demanding $50 million in ransom.

## Region Wise Ransomware Threat Landscape

### United States
The U.S. led all countries with 1,814 attacks (57% of the global total). Construction and Professional Services were the most targeted sectors.

### APAC
Taiwan was the most targeted country (37 incidents), followed by Singapore (32), India (28), and Japan (24). Qilin was the most prolific threat actor in this region.

### Europe and United Kingdom
A total of 663 ransomware attacks were recorded. Germany and the U.K. were the hardest hit. Akira was the most active group in the region with nearly 100 attacks.

### Australia and New Zealand (ANZ)
Ransomware threats doubled year-over-year, with 64 total attacks. Healthcare, professional services, and SMEs faced the brunt of these campaigns.

### Middle East and Africa
The UAE and South Africa were top targets. Groups like Everest, RansomHub, and Lynx focused on IT, construction, and energy sectors.

## New Ransomware Groups
- **Dire Wolf**: Emerged with an onion-based leak site, listing six victims across Asia, Australia, and Italy.
- **DATACARRY**: Targeting European companies; seven victims listed.
- **“J”**: Emerged in March 2025, targeting organizations across South America, Australia, Europe, the U.S., and Asia.
- **Silent Team**: Claimed two victims (U.S. and Canada), exfiltrating 2.85 TB of data.
- **Gunra**: Listed three victims in Japan, Egypt, and Panama.

## Software Supply Chain Attacks Surged in the Second Quarter
Cyble documented 105 cyberattacks with supply chain implications in the first six months of 2025. Attacks increased from an average of 13 per month (Feb–Sept 2024) to over 17 per month (Oct 2024–June 2025).

---

## Software Supply Chain Attacks: Targeted Industries and Countries
62% of supply chain attacks targeted IT, technology, and telecommunications companies. The U.S. was the target in 42 of the 105 incidents, followed by Europe (32) and APAC (36).

---

## Notable Supply Chain Incidents
Cyble documented 10 significant incidents, including attacks by Everest, Akira, Hellcat, DragonForce, VanHelsing, Crypto24, Killsec, and Medusa, targeting sectors ranging from banking and telecommunications to biometric recognition and cloud infrastructure.

## Hacktivism Gets More Sophisticated
Hacktivism in 2025 moved beyond DDoS attacks into ransomware and critical infrastructure attacks.

### Critical Infrastructure Hacktivism
- **Z-Pentest**: Russia-linked group responsible for 38 ICS attacks in Q2 2025.
- **Dark Engine**: A new group that accounted for 26 ICS-targeted incidents in Q2, focusing on national resilience sectors.
- **Sector 16**: Linked to 14 attacks in the most recent quarter, often collaborating with Z-Pentest.

## Conclusion
H1 2025 marked a clear escalation in both volume and sophistication of ransomware attacks. From zero-day abuse and sector-specific targeting to the rise of multi-continent hacktivism and software supply chain threats, organizations face a landscape that demands proactive threat intelligence, robust patching hygiene, and cross-sector cyber resilience strategies.
