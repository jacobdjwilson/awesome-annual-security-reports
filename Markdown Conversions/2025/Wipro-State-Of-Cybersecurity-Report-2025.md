## Foreword

### A note from Tony Buffomante
**SVP & Global Head — Cybersecurity & Risk Services, Wipro Ltd.**

AI is driving continual innovation and disruption — acting as both friend and foe at a time when CISOs are under financial pressure to do more with less.

Digital infrastructure transformation remains a top priority for today’s business leaders. Increased technological complexity, constantly evolving regulations and a rise in sophisticated cyber threats across multi-hybrid cloud environments create daunting challenges for security teams. CISOs need to adopt a risk-adjusted, outcome-oriented mindset and transition from technologists who merely prevent and react to breaches to risk strategists focused on optimizing enterprise cyber resilience.

This report answers three key questions:
1. What is the current state of cyberattacks and breaches?
2. What is the state of enterprise capabilities for addressing evolving threats?
3. Which technologies are poised to impact enterprise cybersecurity in the near future?

Two big themes are at the forefront of these efforts:
- **AI and automation**: AI is driving exponential productivity gains, enhancing threat detection, automating repetitive tasks, and remediating security vulnerabilities.
- **Cost optimization**: CISOs face growing pressure to maximize security investments with limited financial resources.

We have made a strategic internal investment to develop **CyberTransform℠** — our advisory and implementation services that strengthen security and drive business growth. Our expansion into advisory and consulting perfectly complements our foundational **CyberShield℠** managed services expertise.

## Executive Summary

### The Big Picture
The interplay between AI and cybersecurity will have far-reaching consequences. While AI makes cybersecurity capabilities more efficient, it is a double-edged sword, as AI models and systems will be severely tested by targeted cyberattacks.

**Key Findings:**
- **Nation-state attacks and trade wars ramp up**: 86% of global nation-state attacks focus on intellectual property theft. The private sector has experienced 32% of all public nation-state attacks.
- **AI Enabled Attacks**: Email phishing remains the dominant vector. 31% of organizations that have experienced a breach have fallen victim to repeat breaches within a three-year window.
- **Risk reporting to the board is becoming more frequent**: Organizations are shifting toward proactive cyber governance.
- **AI strengthens and disrupts**: CISOs are evolving their security operations centers (SOCs) based on practical wins where GenAI can be realistically leveraged.
- **Agentic AI & PQC pave the way to the future**: Global AI arms race is heating up, with the US continuing to be the driving force. Filings related to quantum computing and Post-Quantum Cryptography (PQC) are on the rise.

## State of Attacks and Breaches

### Nation-State Cyber Warfare
Nation-state actors are improving their cyber operations skills, creating sophisticated malware, and employing advanced tactics to disable critical infrastructure and acquire sensitive information.

**Data point summary:**
- 86% of all attacks are espionage related.
- 42% of all targets are government entities.
- The main aggressor countries are China, Russia, Iran, and North Korea.
- The most targeted country is the US (12%), followed by Ukraine (11%).

![Sankey graph on global nation-state attacks]

### Breaches – The Data and The Targets
Between 2022 and 2025, there was a notable increase in both the frequency and monetary impact of data breaches.

**Key Trends:**
- 51% of breaches involved advanced PII (an increase of 13% from 2023).
- 16% of breaches involved basic PII.
- 15% of breaches included financial data.

## State of Cyber Capabilities

### Top Cyber Risks
1. **Email Phishing (65%)**: Cybercriminals are increasingly using AI to automate attacks.
2. **Third-Party Risks (49%)**: Interconnected ecosystems amplify vulnerabilities.
3. **Security Awareness / Negligence (44%)**.
4. **Ransomware Attacks (40%)**.

### Board Alignment for Cybersecurity
Proactive cyber governance organizations increased from 49% in 2023 to approximately 64% in 2025. This includes designated board members with cyber risk experience and separate cyber risk committees.

### CISO Reporting
53% of organizations still have their CISOs reporting to the CIO. 22% report directly to the CEO or have regular CEO reviews.

### Security Budget and Investment Priorities
Only 10% of organizations reported allocating more than 12% of their annual IT budget to cybersecurity, compared to 21% in 2023. Organizations are shifting priorities toward "spending better" rather than "spending more."

**Top Investment Priorities:**
- **Zero Trust**: 97%
- **AI-driven threat detection**: 93%
- **IoT device management**: 82%

## Future of Cybersecurity

### Cyber Patent Trends
Since 2019, nearly 46,000 patent families related to cybersecurity have been submitted. 
- China leads in total number of patents filed (over 37,000).
- The U.S. follows with more than 8,000.
- Notably, only 4.7% of Chinese patent families were filed in the U.S. and other international jurisdictions, indicating most are primarily domestic.

### Spotlight: Cyber Investment and Funding Trends
- The total number of cybersecurity deals dropped from approximately 600 in 2021/2022 to 429 in 2023.
- The U.S., Europe, and Israel remain the primary geographies for cybersecurity startups.
- Security market consolidation is expected to continue as large players acquire startups to supplement growth in areas like AI security.
