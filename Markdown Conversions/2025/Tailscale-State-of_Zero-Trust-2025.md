## 00// Introduction
_from Avery Pennarun, CEO of Tailscale_

It’s 2025, and the perimeter is dead.

If your mental model of a secure network still involves “inside” and “outside,” it’s time for an update. Most companies know this — at least, they say they do. Our latest survey suggests Zero Trust has become the new checkbox buzzword. Unfortunately, saying “Zero Trust” and actually doing it are two very different things.

These are my three takeaways from the data:

1. **Everyone's frustrated**: IT teams are overwhelmed by tool sprawl and manual processes. Security leaders are stuck enforcing policies users constantly work around. Engineers just want to get things done — and they say current access setups are too slow, too fragile, and too painful. Different problems, same conclusion: the legacy model isn’t working.
2. **Zero trust is half-deployed at best**: Nearly every organization says they’re “on a Zero Trust journey,” which is a polite way of saying they aren’t done, and maybe never will be. Fewer than a third have implemented the foundational parts — strong identity, least privilege, and verifying before trusting.
3. **There's some light at the end of the tunnel**: There’s real interest in emerging approaches: identity-first networking, modular security systems, and policy engines that adapt (instead of break) when things change. And with AI accelerating both innovation and risk, there’s new urgency to get access right.

## 01// Data transparency

All statistics and insights in this report are based on Tailscale’s 2025 Secure Access and Zero Trust Adoption Survey, conducted with 1,000 IT, security, and engineering professionals. 

### Methodology
Tailscale conducted this study in partnership with Kelsey White and PureSpectrum. The online survey was completed by n=1,000 engineering, security, development, and IT professionals across the United States and Canada.

## 02// Executive summary

### Zero Trust in theory, not in practice
In 2025, organizations are under pressure to secure increasingly distributed, hybrid, and cloud-native environments — but the tools they rely on are frustrating and outdated (especially VPNs). 

### Access and connectivity redesign priorities
- 99% of companies want stronger security.
- 49% want increased speed and performance.
- 45% want systems that scale for growth.
- 38% want to save money.
- 33% want better integration with tools.
- 32% want more automation.
- 30% want better UX/UI.
- 26% want better access controls and visibility.

Only 1% report being satisfied with the status quo.

### Key Findings
- **Identity-based access**: Only 29% use identity-based access as their primary model.
- **Manual processes**: 68% of organizations still rely on manual provisioning for network access.
- **Tool sprawl**: 92% of organizations juggle multiple solutions; only 8% rely on a single, unified platform.
- **Workarounds**: 83% of respondents have bypassed security processes.
- **Offboarding failures**: 68% of respondents report retaining access to former employers’ systems after leaving.

## 03// The push toward identity-native access

The concept of identity-native security — where access to systems is determined by user and device identity, not by network location — has become a pillar of Zero Trust strategies. 

### Reasons for delaying or deprioritizing networking or security upgrades
- 42% Risk of disruption to workflows or integrations.
- 39% Conflicting leadership or organizational priorities.
- 35% Cost or resource constraints.
- 34% Lack of clear business case or uncertain ROI.
- 33% Lack of clear direction or suitable solutions.
- 31% Recent or ongoing rollout of other tools.

---

## 04// Legacy VPNs

Legacy VPNs extend the network perimeter rather than enforcing access based on user or device identity. 

### Current VPN limitations
- 40% Security risks.
- 35% Latency/speed issues.
- 34% High ops overhead.
- 28% Integration challenges.
- 25% Inability to scale.
- 24% Throughput issues.
- 23% Employee complaints.
- 20% Remote growth struggles.
- 19% Cloud/hybrid incompatible.

---

## 05// Productivity vs. security

32% of IT and security professionals say their top challenge is balancing security with speed and productivity. 

### The cultural disconnect
- 51% say their current remote access setup slows them down.
- 51% believe better security must come at the cost of performance.
- 68% of survey respondents hear or have complaints about network access on a weekly or monthly basis.

---

## 06// Security trends and the road ahead

The data points to a shared recognition that the old model is failing. 

- **Automation and AI**: 24% of IT and security teams cite “too much manual work” as a top challenge.
- **Adaptive Security**: A shift away from binary, rule-based access toward risk-aware, context-sensitive decisions (Continuous Adaptive Trust).

1. **Identity will become the foundation of access architecture.**
2. **Implicit trust in networks will disappear.**
3. **VPNs won’t vanish, but they’ll be redefined.**
4. **AI will power both defense and attack.**

6. **Security will become part of engineering culture.**

8. **Zero Trust will stretch from login to deployment pipeline.**
9. **Standards will (finally) catch up.**
10. **Zero Trust will extend to edge and IoT environments.**

## 08// Recommendations

1. **Develop an identity-first access strategy**: Make identity the linchpin of your access control model.
2. **Accelerate VPN replacement plans**: Pilot a Zero Trust Network Access (ZTNA) solution.
3. **Tighten onboarding/offboarding processes**: Establish a zero-tolerance policy for ex-employees retaining access.
4. **Consolidate and integrate tools**: Reduce complexity by consolidating overlapping security tools.
5. **Improve user experience**: Security should be mostly invisible.
6. **Enable and educate**: Turn frustrated users into allies via a Security Champions program.
7. **Implement Just-In-Time (JIT) and least privilege access**: Do not grant standing access unless necessary.
8. **Boost monitoring and incident response**: Strengthen monitoring of access patterns and alert systems.
9. **Plan for scalability**: Design secure access with scalability in mind using cloud-delivered services.
10. **Align with a Zero Trust framework**: Use NIST 800-207 or CISA’s Zero Trust Maturity Model to assess progress.
