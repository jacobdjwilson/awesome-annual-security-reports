## Introduction
We believe that building and operationalizing AI products is the new frontier of competitive advantage – and that the voices of the architects, engineers, and product leaders driving this work deserve their own spotlight. While last year’s State of AI report centered on the buying journey and enterprise adoption dynamics, our 2025 report pivots squarely to the “how-to”: what it takes to conceive, deliver, and scale AI-powered offerings end-to-end.

This year’s report unpacks core dimensions of the builder’s playbook:
1. **Product Roadmap & Architecture**: The emerging best practices for balancing experimentation, speed to market, and performance at each stage of model evolution.
2. **Go-to-Market Strategy**: How teams are aligning pricing models and go-to-market strategies to reflect AI’s unique value drivers.
3. **People & Talent**: Building the right team to harness AI expertise, foster cross-functional collaboration, and sustain long-term innovation.
4. **Cost Management & ROI**: Strategies and benchmarks for spend associated with building and launching AI products.
5. **Internal Productivity & Operations**: How companies are embedding AI into everyday workflows and the biggest drivers of productivity unlock.

## Data, Respondent Firmographics, and Methodology
This study summarizes data from an April 2025 survey of 300 executives at software companies building AI products, including CEOs, Heads of Engineering, Heads of AI, and Heads of Product.

![Chart showing respondent revenue range and headquarters distribution]

**High-Growth Companies Criteria:**
- **AI Product Traction**: AI product is in General Availability or Scaling.
- **Revenue**: At least $10M in annual revenue.
- **Topline Growth**: 100%+ YoY revenue growth if <$25M Revenue, 50%+ YoY revenue growth if $25M-250M Revenue, 30%+ YoY revenue growth if $250M+ Revenue.

## AI Maturity
Most SaaS companies have evolved to add new AI capabilities and products.

- **Traditional SaaS**: Delivery of subscription-based applications built around core business workflows.
- **AI-Enabled (Features)**: Embedded AI-powered features into flagship offerings.
- **AI-Enabled (New Product)**: Standalone AI-driven product or services alongside core product portfolio.
- **AI-Native**: Entire value proposition is architected around generative intelligence.

## Building GenAI Products
### Stage of Primary AI Product
AI-native companies are further along in the development cycle, with 47% of products having reached critical scale.
![Chart comparing AI-Enabled vs AI-Native development stages]

### Types of AI Products
- **Agentic workflows**: 79% (AI-Native) / 40% (AI-Enabled)
- **Vertical AI applications**: 65% (AI-Native) / 57% (AI-Enabled)
- **Horizontal AI applications**: 62% (AI-Native) / 48% (AI-Enabled)
- **AI platforms / infrastructure**: 56% (AI-Native) / 27% (AI-Enabled)
- **Core AI models / technologies**: 55% (AI-Native) / 49% (AI-Enabled)

### Model Usage
80% of high-growth companies rely on third-party APIs, while 77% fine-tune existing foundation models, and 61% develop proprietary models from scratch.

### Top Considerations for Foundational Models
1. **Accuracy**: 74%
2. **Cost**: 57%
3. **Ability to fine-tune / customize**: 41%

## AI Costs
### Budget Allocation
As products move from Beta to Scaling, the percentage of budget allocated to **AI infrastructure & cloud costs** increases from 20% to 24%, while **AI talent** spend as a proportion of the total budget decreases.

### Infrastructure Cost Challenges
1. **API usage fees**: 70%
2. **Inference costs**: 49%
3. **Model retraining and updates**: 48%

## AI Builder Tech Stack
### Model Training & Finetuning
- **Frameworks**: PyTorch and TensorFlow remain dominant.
- **Managed Platforms**: AWS SageMaker and OpenAI’s fine-tuning service are widely used.

### LLM & AI Application Development
- **Orchestration**: LangChain and Hugging Face toolsets are the industry standard.
- **Safety**: 30% of respondents use Guardrails.

### Monitoring and Observability
- **Incumbent Infrastructure**: Nearly half of teams use existing APM/logging stacks (Datadog, Honeycomb, New Relic).
- **ML-Native**: LangSmith and Weights & Biases have reached ~17% adoption.

### Inference Optimization
- **NVIDIA Stack**: TensorRT and Triton Inference Server command >60% adoption.
- **Hardware-Agnostic**: ONNX Runtime (18%) is the leading alternative.

### Model Hosting
- **Direct-from-Provider**: OpenAI, Anthropic, etc., remain the primary path.
- **Hyperscalers**: AWS Bedrock and Google Vertex AI are preferred by later-stage enterprises.

### Model Evaluation
- **Fragmented**: Many teams rely on built-in platform features (Vertex, W&B) or custom-built pipelines.
- **Emerging**: LangSmith and Langfuse are gaining traction for prompt-level metrics.

### Data Processing & Feature Engineering
- **Big Data**: Apache Spark (44%) and Kafka (42%) lead for ETL and ingestion.
- **Python**: Pandas (41%) remains the standard for smaller datasets and prototyping.
