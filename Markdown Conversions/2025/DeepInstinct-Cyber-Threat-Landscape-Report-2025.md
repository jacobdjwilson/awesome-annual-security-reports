## Introduction
In 2024, we witnessed a surge in sophisticated cyber threats. Driven by digitization, the number of ransomware attacks and breaches continued to grow across multiple industries. Threat actors more frequently exploited zero-day vulnerabilities, targeting both public and private sectors, and leveraged AI to automate reconnaissance, vulnerability exploitation, data exfiltration, and malware generation.

Additionally, Ransomware-as-a-Service (RaaS) has continued to evolve, with a marked rise in AI-generated phishing campaigns that bypassed traditional security measures. In response, organizations adopted more proactive defense mechanisms, including deep learning–based threat detection and real-time anomaly detection.

Key events shaping the threat landscape included the global surge in supply chain attacks and several massive, high-profile breaches, exposing millions of global users’ sensitive data. In particular, the healthcare and financial sectors faced unprecedented challenges during 2024, with ransomware groups using advanced and constantly evolving malware that adapted in real time to evade signature-based defenses. Additionally, several zero-day vulnerabilities in widely used enterprise software were exploited, underscoring the need for timely patch management and threat intelligence sharing.

Collaborations between cybersecurity firms and government agencies led to the successful takedown of a major ransomware network in late 2024, highlighting the power of global cooperation in cybersecurity. Despite these notable successes, cyberattacks still rose during the final months of 2024, as they do at the end of each year.

The trends observed this year emphasize the need for a paradigm shift in data security strategies. Organizations must adopt a layered strategy that features preemptive cybersecurity capabilities, combining deep learning models for predictive threat analysis and prevention to effectively fight back against Dark AI. Proactive threat hunting powered by AI-driven insights and continuously improving threat intelligence platforms is crucial in mitigating future threats. As adversaries refine their tactics, the focus must shift to proactive prevention and zero-day data security frameworks to safeguard sensitive data.

## The Top Malware Trends of 2024
As cyber threats continued to evolve, 2024 experienced a surge in malware activity, with ransomware remaining a dominant force. While the overall number of attacks has increased, the rate of growth slowed compared to previous years, reflecting both the persistence of cybercriminal tactics and the growing sophistication of organizational defenses.

## Ransomware Trends
The total number of ransomware attacks in 2024 was higher than in previous years, but the growth rate slowed, especially compared to the dramatic growth between 2022 and 2023. Between 2022 and 2023, ransomware incidents skyrocketed due to a confluence of factors, including increased digitization, more sophisticated attack tools, and more widespread targeting of critical infrastructure. Global ransomware payouts also increased significantly.

Reports show a 30% increase in overall global cyberattacks in Q2 2024 compared to the same period in 2023[^1].

![Figure 1: Ransomware attacks 2024 - A line graph showing a steady increase in attacks throughout the year.]

### Ransomware Surge
Industry data reveals a steady increase in ransomware victims from 2022 to 2024, with 2024 showing the highest numbers in Q1, Q2, and Q3, underscoring the growing severity of the threat. The number of victims in Q2 and Q3 of 2024 was more than double the 2022 numbers during those same quarters. Notably, across all three years, the highest victim counts were consistently in Q4, indicating a surge in attacks toward the end of the year.

## Ransomware Threat Actors
The majority of prominent ransomware groups in 2024 operated under a RaaS model, allowing them to scale operations by recruiting affiliates who conduct attacks using the group’s infrastructure and tools in exchange for a percentage of ransom payments.

![Figure 5: Top ransomware threat actors of 2024 - A bar chart listing groups like Lockbit3, Ransomhub, and Dispossessor.]
![Figure 6: Top ransomware threat actors of 2024 with comparison to their all-time activity - A comparative bar chart.]

### Top Ransomware Groups in 2024
- **Lockbit 3.0:** A sophisticated group known for double extortion and a highly successful RaaS affiliate program.
- **RansomHub:** A rapidly evolving group that emerged as an iteration of the Knight ransomware operation.
- **Dispossessor:** Primarily functioned as a data broker rather than a direct ransomware operator.
- **Play:** A financially motivated cluster known for stealthy infiltration and double-extortion tactics.
- **Hunters International:** Emerged in late 2023; known for the SharpRhino RAT.
- **Medusa:** Maintains a presence on both the dark web and public web; utilizes RMM tools for persistence.
- **Akira:** Active throughout 2024; utilizes both C++ and Rust-based encryptors.
- **Qilin:** Suspected Russian-speaking group; known for high-value targets and political motivation.
- **BianLian:** Shifted from encryption-based extortion to exclusively data-theft extortion in 2024.
- **Black Basta:** Known for social engineering campaigns targeting users via phone and Microsoft Teams.
- **BlackSuit:** Shares code similarities with Royal ransomware; targets healthcare and education.
- **8Base:** Employs a "name-and-shame" tactic on its leak site.

## Top Banking Trojans/Stealers/Spyware
- **Pony:** A dangerous password-stealing malware targeting VPNs, FTP, and email.
- **Cryxos:** Trojan Scareware that uses JavaScript to display fake tech support alerts.
- **Berbew:** Designed to steal user passwords and act as a proxy for C2 functions.
- **Grandoreiro:** A banking Trojan targeting 1,800 banks across 50 countries; uses mouse-movement recording to bypass ML security.

## Other Notable Malware
- **Cobalt Strike Beacon:** A commercial penetration testing tool misused for malicious payloads.
- **Expiro:** File-infecting malware that embeds into legitimate executables.
- **IFramer:** Silently injects hidden iframes into compromised websites.
- **Snojan:** Disguises itself within legitimate software to steal sensitive data.
- **Cosmu:** Provides attackers with unauthorized remote control over systems.

## The Rise of Data Brokers as an Adaptation to Sanctions and Disclosures
As enforcement measures target ransomware groups, the ecosystem adapts by employing specialized data brokers to handle stolen data, reducing operational complexity for smaller criminal crews.

## The Rise in Cyberattacks Driven by Geopolitical Events
Cyber operations have become a critical theater of modern warfare.
- **Israel–Hamas War:** Cyberterrorism and DoS attacks targeting critical infrastructure.
- **Russia–Ukraine War:** Intensified cyber campaigns against Ukraine and NATO allies, including the UK.
- **China–Taiwan Tension:** Cyberattacks used to influence political landscapes and weaken defenses.

## AI-Based Attacks
AI and LLMs are being exploited to automate reconnaissance, generate polymorphic malware, and craft highly personalized phishing campaigns. The dual-use nature of AI makes it a significant challenge for traditional security tools.

## Vulnerabilities on the Rise
There were 186 known exploited vulnerabilities in 2024. Nearly 20% were in Microsoft products. A concerning trend is the increased exploitation of vulnerabilities in leading network security and IT solutions.

## Predictions
1. **AI-Driven Attacks:** A rise in "unknown" malware that evades signature-based detection.
2. **Startup-like Operations:** Cybercrime groups will continue to adopt corporate structures to increase efficiency.
3. **Increased Attack Surface:** Digital transformation and the growth of global data volume will provide more targets.
4. **IoT-Based Attacks:** A surge in attacks on connected devices (e.g., Mirai, Gafgyt, IOCONTROL).
5. **Dual-Use RMM Tools:** Increased use of legitimate remote management tools to disguise malicious activity.

## Conclusion
The cybersecurity landscape of 2024 was marked by significant evolution in both attack sophistication and defensive measures. Looking ahead, organizations must prepare for an increasingly complex threat environment. Success will require a paradigm shift in security strategies, emphasizing preemptive threat postures and zero-day data security frameworks.
