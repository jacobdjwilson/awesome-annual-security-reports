For consumers and businesses alike, voice communication remains absolutely critical. In fact, the number of consumers and businesses who say that voice calls are essential, especially in situations involving sensitive information, has only increased in recent years. Even in an increasingly digital world, people crave the human connection that voice provides.

Yet at the same time, individuals and businesses report persistent struggles in making calls reliably and securely:

- Individuals worry about their ability to trust voice communication in an era of rampant spam and fraud calls.
- For businesses, reaching consumers by phone is increasingly difficult as scammers impersonate brands, damaging trust and deliverability. At the same time, organizations themselves are frequent targets of attacks, from voice-phishing to social engineering.

These challenges reflect long-standing voice security and trust issues, such as spam and fraud calls. However, they have been exacerbated over the past year by newer, more sophisticated threats, such as AI-powered deepfakes that are harder to detect than conventional phone scam techniques.

> **80% of unidentified calls are likely to never be answered.**

The result is much more than mere annoyance or inconvenience. Consumers and businesses are losing money when they fall victim to voice scams, or when they feel they can no longer rely on voice calls for secure and effective communication.

The good news: AI-powered solutions already exist to tackle many of these challenges, improving the phone experience by boosting both security and productivity.

## Understanding unwanted calls

At Hiya, we use the term “unwanted call” to refer to a voice call that originates from outside a recipient’s contact list that consumers don’t want to receive. Unwanted calls range from illegal fraud attempts to steal money or personal data, to legal but irritating nuisance calls that frustrate consumers.

Unwanted calls are sometimes labeled as spam or fraud on consumers’ mobile devices. However, many of the calls that consumers deem unwanted are not labeled at all. Nearly half—48 percent—never answer unidentified calls, and another third–33 percent–rarely answer them. This means a whopping 80 percent of unidentified calls are likely to never be answered.

In the case of the other half of unidentified calls—those that consumers do pick up–recipients typically only answer reluctantly, due to concerns that it may be a call they can’t miss. Only 20 percent of consumers report that they always answer unidentified calls.

## Top voice call trends from 2025

Key findings from this year’s State of the Call Survey:

- **Voice remains a critical communication medium:** Across most types of communication categories, use of voice calls remained the same or increased. 40 percent of businesses are looking to add a branded calling solution in 2025.
- **Significant concern about security:** Spam calls, unknown caller IDs, and voice scams create major challenges—prompting 60 percent of IT departments to plan call protection investments in 2025.
- **The surging deepfake threat:** 39 percent of consumers and nearly half of employees report having been impacted by a deepfake attack in just a single recent three-month period.
- **Serious financial impact:** 15 percent of consumers say they lost money to a phone scam in 2024, and about one-third of workers report that voice calling challenges cost their business money.
- **Under-utilization of protection:** Only a third of employees use personal spam detection apps, and most companies adopt call confirmation tech only after falling victim to a scam.

## Trend 1: The criticality of voice calls

In domains like finance and healthcare, where communication often requires sharing private information, the strong majority of consumers report that voice is their preferred communication medium. 

**Consumer communication preferences:**
- **Banking:** 33% Voice, 23% Email, 7% Text
- **Healthcare:** 42% Voice, 18% Email, 7% Text
- **Retail:** 28% Voice, 26% Email, 8% Text

## Trend 2: Persistent concerns about voice security

The challenges consumers face surrounding voice security center in large part on unidentified calls:
- Fewer than 20% of consumers say they always answer unidentified calls.
- 32% sometimes answer unidentified calls.
- 48% never answer unidentified calls.

**Business impact:**
- 28% of workers say their companies’ outbound calls are at least sometimes marked as spam.
- 26% of workers have experienced the use of their business’s name by fraud callers.
- 27% of workers have experienced call spoofing.

## Trend 3: The surging deepfake threat

A deepfake uses AI to convincingly mimic someone’s voice or image. During a three-month period starting in late 2024, 39 percent of consumers reported experiencing a deepfake communication.

**Where consumers encounter deepfakes most often:**
- Voice call: 13%
- Text message: 12%
- Video: 11%

**Hiya Honeypot Data (Q1 2025):**
- 25% of calls in the honeypot contained AI-generated audio.
- Of those, 55% were identified as fraud and 35% as nuisance.

## Trend 4: The financial fallout of calling challenges

- 15% of consumers say they lost money to a phone scam in the last year.
- 33% of workers say their company has lost money because they couldn’t reach customers by phone.
- 33% of people say they are considering switching services due to how their mobile carrier handles spam and fraud calls.

## Trend 5: Under-investment in call protection solutions

Two-thirds of consumers reported using no call protection apps. For businesses, while 74 percent report investing in some form of anti-deepfake protection, many are not yet acting as proactively as they could.

## Spotlight: The BYOD challenge

IT leaders reported that about 68% of businesses have a BYOD (Bring Your Own Device) policy. While many organizations have taken steps to secure these personal devices, only around half have implemented inbound anti-fraud authentication.

## Recommendations

**For Businesses:**
- **Business number registration:** Establish legitimacy with mobile carriers.
- **Branded caller ID:** Display identity to increase answer rates.
- **Inbound call protections:** Deploy technology to monitor for incoming threats.
- **Adopt consumer-friendly calling practices:** Avoid repetitive, unwanted calls.

**For Consumers:**
- **Install call protection apps:** Use tools that detect spam, fraud, and deepfakes.
- **Choose a secure carrier:** Look for providers with strong, AI-powered fraud prevention.
- **Report unwanted calls:** Use built-in tools or third-party apps to flag threats.

## Conclusion: Making the world safe for voice calling

The data is clear: The world can’t live without voice calling. Yet, too often, voice calls present challenges due to traditional risks like number spoofing as well as more novel threats like deepfakes. Solutions are available, but stakeholders must take action to deploy them to preserve voice as a safe, reliable, and trustworthy means of communication.

## Methodology

- **Market Research:** Censuswide conducted research between Dec 24, 2024, and Jan 7, 2025, surveying 12,003 consumers, 1,802 workers, and 600 senior IT/Security professionals across the UK, US, Canada, France, Germany, and Spain.
- **Hiya Data:** Based on analysis of more than 262.8 billion calls passing over the Hiya network in 2024. Data is aggregated and anonymized.
