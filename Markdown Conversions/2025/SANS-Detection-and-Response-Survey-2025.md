## Key Findings

*   **Cloud complexity outpaces expertise.** Cloud detection continues to challenge defenders, with limited cloud security expertise and multicloud complexity as growing barriers.
*   **False positives surge as a persistent pain point.** False positives remain the leading operational burden, suggesting that tuning and precision have not kept pace with expanded detection coverage.
*   **Budgets under strain despite rising demands.** Funding pressures are mounting, with budgets that are insufficient, revealing a widening gap between operational expectations and resources.
*   **Automation is on the rise.** More organizations are automating detection and response workflows to offset staffing shortages.
*   **Skill and resource gaps persist.** Teams continue to face constraints around staffing, expertise, and budget.
*   **AI moves from experimentation to execution.** Plans to expand AI and machine learning (ML) surged, marking a shift from proof-of-concept to production use.
*   **Future outlook is cautious but innovative.** Investment in AI-driven threat hunting, predictive analytics, and orchestration signals a strategic push toward adaptive security operations.

## Introduction

The 2025 Detection and Response Survey provides an in-depth look at how organizations are evolving their cybersecurity operations in the face of mounting complexity and resource constraints. The findings highlight a growing emphasis on automation, integration, and measurable performance, balanced by an ongoing reliance on skilled human judgment.

## Detection

X/EDR solutions remain the cornerstone of modern detection, with 43% of respondents rating them as extremely effective. AI- and ML-based tools continue to struggle to inspire confidence, with only 16% finding them extremely effective.

## Machine Learning

Just over half of respondents (53%) reported using ML algorithms for threat detection. While adoption is steady, 39% now rate ML as “very useful,” a significant jump from 22% last year.

## Conclusion

The 2025 survey reveals a community that is evolving to meet threats by blending automation, human expertise, and emerging technologies. While challenges like false positives and resource constraints persist, the shift toward proactive, intelligence-driven operations suggests a more resilient future for security teams.
