## Introduction
Ponemon Institute is pleased to present the findings of the 2022 Cost of Insider Threats Global Report.

> THIS IS THE FOURTH BENCHMARK STUDY CONDUCTED WITH THE EXPLICIT PURPOSE TO UNDERSTAND THE FINANCIAL CONSEQUENCES THAT RESULT FROM INSIDER THREATS. A SECONDARY FOCUS IS TO GAIN INSIGHT INTO HOW WELL ORGANIZATIONS ARE MITIGATING THESE RISKS.

The first Cost of Insider Threats: Global study was conducted in 2016 and focused exclusively on companies in North America. Since then, the research has expanded to include organizations in Europe, Middle East, Africa and Asia-Pacific with a global headcount of 500 to more than 75,000. In this year’s study, we interviewed 1,004 IT and IT security practitioners in 278 organizations that experienced one or more material events caused by an insider. A total of 6,803 insider incidents are represented in this research.

In the context of this research, insider threats are defined as:
- A careless or negligent employee or contractor
- A criminal or malicious insider
- A credential thief

## Executive Summary
INSIDER THREATS HAVE INCREASED IN BOTH FREQUENCY AND COST OVER THE PAST TWO YEARS. CREDENTIAL THEFTS, FOR EXAMPLE, HAVE ALMOST DOUBLED IN NUMBER SINCE 2020.

However, despite insider threats having increased across all three insider threat profiles, insider threats caused by careless or negligent employees are the most prevalent. According to the findings, 56% of incidents experienced by organizations represented in this research were due to negligence, and the average annual cost to remediate the incident was $6.6 million.

![Infographic showing 278 benchmarked organizations, 56% negligence incidents, $6.6M annual cost for negligence, 6,803 total incidents, 26% criminal insider incidents, $4.1M annual cost for criminal insider, $15.4M total average annual cost, 18% credential theft incidents, and $4.6M annual cost for credential theft.]

### Key Statistics
- **The time to contain an insider incident increased**: It took an average of 85 days to contain the incident, an increase from 77 days in the previous study. Only 12% of incidents were contained in less than 30 days.
- **The negligent insider is the root cause of most incidents**: 3,807 attacks (56%) were caused by negligence, costing on average $484,931 per incident.
- **Malicious insiders**: Caused 26% (1,749) of incidents at an average cost of $648,062 per incident.
- **Credential theft**: Incidents have almost doubled since the last study. At an average of $804,997 per incident, it is the costliest to remediate.
- **Frequency**: 67% of companies are experiencing between 21 and more than 40 incidents per year.
- **Business Disruption (23%) and Technology (21%)**: Represent the most significant costs.

### 05 Signs that your organization is at risk
1. Employees are not trained to fully understand and apply laws, mandates, or regulatory requirements.
2. Employees are unaware of the steps they should take to ensure devices (company-issued and BYOD) are secured.
3. Employees are sending highly confidential data to an unsecured location in the cloud.
4. Employees break security policies to simplify tasks.
5. Employees do not keep devices and services patched and upgraded.

## About the Study
Our research focuses on actual insider-related events or incidents that impact organizational costs over the past 12 months. Methods utilize an activity-based costing (ABC) framework. Fieldwork was conducted over a two-month period concluding in September 2021.

## Key Findings
- **Frequency**: 56% of incidents were employee/contractor negligence, 26% criminal/malicious, and 18% credential theft.
- **Trends**: Credential theft incidents increased from an average of 3.2 in 2020 to 5.7 in 2022.
- **Containment**: 85 days average to contain.
- **Tools**: Top technologies for cost reduction are Data Loss Prevention (DLP), Privileged Access Management (PAM), and User and Entity Behavior Analytics (UEBA).

---

## The Cost of Insider Incidents
- **Cost Categories**: Disruption (23%), Technology (21%), Direct & indirect labor (19%), Process/workflow changes (13%), Cash outlays (12%), Revenue losses (7%), Overhead (5%).
- **Credential Theft**: Remains the costliest incident type at $804,997 per incident.

---

## Cost Analysis
- **Activity Centers**: Monitoring & surveillance, Investigation, Escalation, Incident response, Containment, Ex-post analysis, and Remediation.
- **Containment**: Represents the highest cost center (29%).
- **Regional Costs**: North American companies spend the most ($17.53M) on resolving insider threats.

---

## Managing the Insider Threat
- **Concerns**: 55% of respondents are most concerned about credential theft.
- **Data Storage**: 65% of respondents state employees store sensitive data in email.
- **Advanced Tech**: 62% of respondents consider user behavior-based tools essential or very important.

---

## Conclusions
The rapid digital transformation has increased insider risk. Organizations should implement a people-centric Insider Threat Management (ITM) program focusing on:
1. **Visibility**: Context into data movement.
2. **Consistency**: Repeatable processes to detect and respond.
3. **Transparency**: Continuous improvement and lessons learned.

---

## Framework
This research uses an activity-based costing (ABC) framework to measure the full cost impact of insider incidents, categorizing costs into internal activity centers and external consequences (information loss, business disruption, equipment damage, and lost revenue).

---

## Benchmarking
The study relies on confidential diagnostic interviews and numerical estimation based on the knowledge and experience of participants. Costs were annualized based on a four-week data collection period.

---

## Research Limitations
- **Non-statistical results**: The sample is representative but non-statistical.
- **Non-response**: Non-response bias was not tested.
- **Sampling-frame bias**: Likely biased toward companies with mature security programs.
- **Extrapolated cost results**: Relies on respondent estimates rather than actual accounting data.
