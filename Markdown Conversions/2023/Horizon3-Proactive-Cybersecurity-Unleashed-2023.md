## Challenge 1: Credentials are Still the Number One Issue

As the trend remains from last year, cyber threat actors don’t typically use sophisticated hacking tools and techniques like zero-day exploits to gain access to a network; they simply log in with legitimated user credentials. Once they gain initial access, threat actors then appear as legitimate users and can move laterally within a network to gain further access and establish persistence, steal sensitive data, bring down systems, and/or hold the organization hostage through ransomware.

### But How Did They Get in to My Admin Account?

One primary reason for this issue is pre-configured default username and passwords loaded on devices and systems when they arrive from the manufacturer. Additionally, there’s a tendency among administrative users to choose weak passwords or reuse the same passwords across multiple accounts.

> "Some things like service accounts and similar had slipped through the cracks. Our IT and admin accounts were not subject to quarterly password resets, so NodeZero helped us figure out those accounts, keeping us in the know." — *Highlighted by one of our customers in a recent case study.*

### Implications of Credential-based Attacks

NodeZero continues to achieve critical impacts across multiple pentests within every industry, daily. These critical impacts include domain compromise, host compromise, sensitive data exposure, critical infrastructure compromise, or ransomware exposure.

![Figure 5: Attack path showing host, domain admin, and domain compromise using an injected cleartext password and the NodeZero RAT.]

### Policy Recommendations and Mitigation Actions

- **Training**: Increase training for employees on the dangers of credential reuse and weak passwords.
- **LAPS**: Implement a Local Administrator Password Solution (LAPS) for privileged admins.
- **NIST Standards**: Follow NIST Special Publication 800-63B (12+ characters, no breached passwords, no dictionary terms).
- **MFA**: Require multifactor authentication for external environments and segmented networks.
- **Configuration Management**: Ensure default credentials are changed before production deployment.
- **Least Privilege**: Adhere to the principle of "least privilege."
- **Account Management**: Disable accounts of former employees.
- **Verification**: Verify implementation using NodeZero AD Password Audit or Phishing Impact tests.

## Challenge 2: Unpatched and Misconfigured Software Are Prime Targets for Cyber Attackers

Throughout the past year, numerous Horizon3.ai customers have conducted pentests, only to discover that their systems are riddled with recent exploitable vulnerabilities and some dating back several years.

> "We had just completed our own penetration test and I was super underwhelmed. Our scoring was low and there was nothing critical to report. Then we kicked off NodeZero, did a scan of our environment, and within a few hours we found a system that was not fully configured. As a result, NodeZero was able to compromise it, then move laterally through the environment, and ended up compromising our whole domain." — *Calvin Engen, CTO at F12.net*

Much like last year, NodeZero continues to find and exploit misconfigurations and vulnerabilities in critical DevOps tools (i.e., Jenkins, GitLab, Kubernetes, and Docker), routers, servers, Integrated Lights Out (iLOs), and Integrated Dell Remote Access (iDRACs).

### Legacy Systems Continue to Remain a Threat

Although NodeZero keeps pace with emerging threats, legacy vulnerabilities still plague organizations every day.

![Figure 10: Attack path shows NodeZero discovery of CVE-2017-0144 leading to host compromise and sensitive data exposure.]
![Figure 11: Sankey chart highlights top weaknesses and impacts, showing that EternalBlue led to 402 sensitive data exposure and 1060 host compromise impacts.]

### Implications of Failures to Patch and Misconfigurations

1. Difficulty implementing patching policies.
2. Difficulty prioritizing what needs to be fixed.
3. Legacy systems that cannot be patched need to be segmented.
4. IT departments may have issues configuring new hardware.

### Policy Recommendations and Mitigation Actions Misconfigurations

We recommend instituting patching and mitigation policies to keep systems up to date. If patching is not possible, segment the machine from the rest of the environment. Most importantly, verify that patches and fix actions were implemented correctly by running a regular cadence of autonomous pentests with NodeZero.

Horizon3.ai created rapid response alerts to combat Zero-Day and N-Day vulnerabilities by:
1. Integrating attack content into NodeZero.
2. Proactively identifying and notifying customers at risk.
