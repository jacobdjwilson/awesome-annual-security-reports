## Research Methodology

To explore the real-world business impact of cybersecurity in 2023, Sophos commissioned an independent survey of 3,000 leaders responsible for IT/cybersecurity across 14 countries. All respondents were from organizations with between 100 and 5,000 employees. The research was conducted in January and February 2023 by Vanson Bourne.

## Executive Summary

### Situation: Adversaries Are Accelerating, and Defenders Are Unable to Keep Up

The study revealed that today’s reality is a two-speed cybersecurity system with adversaries and defenders moving at different speeds. Through automation, cybercrime “as-a-service” models, stealthy impersonation, and adaptation, adversaries are accelerating and can now execute a wide range of sophisticated attacks at scale. With 94% of organizations experiencing a cyberattack of some form in the last year, all companies – regardless of size or revenue – should assume they will be a target in 2023.

Slowed by a shortage of expertise, an overwhelming volume of alerts, and too much time spent on incident response, defenders are unable to keep up. Operationalizing threat detection and response is difficult for most organizations, with 93% finding the execution of essential security operations tasks challenging.

### Business Impact: The Situation Has Financial, Operational and Resourcing Consequences

The direct financial repercussions of a cyber incident are huge, with the average cost to a small or mid-sized organization to remediate a ransomware attack coming in at $1.4 million[^1]. 

Capacity for IT program delivery is reduced, with 55% of respondents reporting that dealing with cyberthreats has negatively impacted the IT team’s work on other projects. 64% wish the IT team could spend more time on strategic issues and less time on firefighting.

The situation also creates a heavy burden on employees. 57% of IT professionals say that worrying about the organization being hit by a cyberattack sometimes keeps them up at night.

## Active Adversary Attacks Are Now Commonplace

23% of organizations experienced an attack involving an Active Adversary in the last year. These are threat actors who adapt their techniques, tactics, and procedures (TTPs) on the fly using real-time hands-on-keyboard actions.

---

## Understanding Active Adversaries

Blocking active adversaries is not enough to thwart them. They deploy multiple TTPs:
- Exploiting security weaknesses to penetrate organizations and move laterally.
- Abusing legitimate IT tools to avoid triggering detections.
- Modifying their attacks in real-time in response to security controls.

## Cybersecurity 2023: The State of the Defenders

### Top Cyber Risk Concerns

Security control misconfigurations are the most widely reported perceived security risk (27.4%), followed by Zero-day attacks (26.8%) and a shortage of in-house cybersecurity skills/expertise (25%).

---

## Differing Approaches to Alert Investigation

Organizations investigate 48% of their security alerts to identify if they are signs of malicious activity. The median time to detect, investigate, and respond to an alert is nine hours for organizations with 100-3,000 employees, rising to 15 hours for those with 3,001-5,000 employees.

---

## Organizations Lack Essential Security Operations Skills

93% of organizations rate at least one core security operations activity as 'challenging'. Identifying the root cause of the incident is the most widespread issue, with 75% of respondents reporting it as challenging.

---

## Adversaries Have Outpaced Defenders

Over half (52%) of IT professionals say that cyberthreats are now too advanced for their organization to deal with on their own, rising to 64% among small businesses (100-250 employees).

---

## The Business Impact

### Program Delivery Impact
55% of organizations say that dealing with cyberthreats has negatively impacted the IT team’s work on other projects. 64% wish the IT team could spend more time on strategic issues and less time on firefighting.

### Financial and Team Impact
57% of respondents say that worrying about the organization being hit by a cyberattack keeps them up at night. This propensity increases as organization revenue grows, reaching 67% in organizations that turn over $5 billion or more.

---

## Recommendations

Addressing the situation requires a three-step approach:
1. **Optimize Prevention**: Stop attacks at the earliest possible point.
2. **Reduce Exposure**: Minimize opportunities for adversaries to exploit gaps.
3. **Disrupt Attackers**: Buy defenders time to respond in the event of an advanced attack.

---

## Accelerating the Defender Flywheel

Organizations can accelerate their cybersecurity flywheel by combining security technologies and human expertise. 78% of organizations plan to add EDR/XDR tools in the coming year, and 44% plan to adopt Managed Detection and Response (MDR) services.
