## Introduction
The landscape of insider risk management continues to evolve rapidly, driven by increasing complexities in IT environments, the adoption of hybrid work models, and the rise in adoption of sophisticated GenAI tools by knowledge workers. This 2024 Insider Threat Report is based on the insights of 413 IT and cybersecurity professionals to capture the latest trends, challenges, and best practices in managing insider threats, providing a comprehensive overview of how organizations are addressing these critical security challenges today.

**KEY FINDINGS INCLUDE:**

- **Rising Frequency and Cost of Attacks**: 48% of organizations reported that insider attacks have become more frequent over the past 12 months. Additionally, 51% experienced six or more attacks in the past year, with the average cost of remediation exceeding $1 million for 29% of respondents.
- **Drivers of Insider Attacks**: The top three drivers behind the surge in insider attacks are complex IT environments (39%), adoption of new technologies (37%), and inadequate security measures (33%), highlighting multifaceted areas of concern that organizations must address.
- **Increased Vulnerability Perception**: A substantial 71% of organizations feel at least moderately vulnerable to insider threats, indicating heightened awareness and concern over internal risks.
- **Unified Visibility and Control**: While 93% of respondents consider unified visibility and control across environments to be critically important, only 36% have a fully integrated solution that delivers unified visibility, underscoring the need for more cohesive security strategies.
- **Tools Gap**: While 50% of respondents have a partially integrated solution, 28% of organizations still rely on limited integration, managing visibility through separate, non-integrated tools, 17% have altogether insufficient tooling, and 20% use disparate systems for monitoring users, applications, and devices, revealing a significant tools gap.
- **Obstacles to Implementation**: Technical challenges (39%) and cost factors (31%) remain the primary obstacles to implementing effective insider threat management tools, although organizations are increasingly recognizing the ROI of investing in advanced security solutions.

## Rising Tide of Insider Attacks
![Chart showing the number of insider attacks experienced in the last 12 months (2023 vs 2024) and a chart showing the change in frequency of attacks over the last 12 months.]

The survey results show a concerning increase in reported incidents from 2023 to 2024. In 2024, only 17% of organizations reported no insider attacks, a significant decrease from 40% in 2023. The number of organizations experiencing 6-10 attacks nearly doubled to 25% from 13%, and those reporting 11-20 attacks saw a dramatic increase to 21% from just 4% in 2023.

## Costly Consequences of Insider Attacks
![Chart showing the estimated average cost of remediation after an insider attack.]

When asked to estimate the average cost of remediation after an insider attack, the most common response, noted by 32% of organizations, was an estimated cost in the range of $100K to $499K. These findings underscore the substantial financial impact of insider attacks, with many organizations incurring costs in the hundreds of thousands to millions of dollars.

## Slow Recovery from Insider Attacks
![Chart showing the time taken to recover from an insider attack.]

The survey reveals that while 55% of organizations report recovering from an insider attack within a day, a significant 45% face recovery times extending up to a week or longer. This highlights a concerning trend—many organizations may be underprepared for the complexities of insider attacks.

## Drivers Behind Insider Attack Surge
![Chart showing the main drivers and enablers behind the increase in insider attacks.]

The most cited reason for the increase in attacks, noted by 39% of respondents, is the increasingly complex IT environment. Close behind, 37% point to technological complexity, such as the adoption of new technologies like IoT and AI. Inadequate security measures were noted by 33% of respondents.

## Challenges in Detecting and Preventing Insider Attacks
![Chart showing the difficulty of detecting and preventing insider attacks compared to external cyber attacks.]

According to the survey, 37% of respondents find detecting and preventing insider attacks more difficult than dealing with external cyber attacks. This reveals a notable improvement from 2023 when 48% of organizations found insider attacks more difficult to detect than external ones.

## Obstacles to Effective Insider Threat Management
![Chart showing the primary obstacles preventing the implementation of effective insider threat management tools and strategies.]

Technical challenges are the leading obstacle to effective insider threat management, cited by 39% of respondents. Cost factors are the second most significant barrier, mentioned by 31% of respondents. Resource limitations (27%) and compliance/privacy concerns (26%) follow.

## Vulnerability to Insider Threats
![Chart showing the perceived vulnerability of organizations to insider threats.]

We asked respondents to rate their organization’s vulnerability to insider threats, and the results show a notable shift in perceptions to being more vulnerable. 71% of organizations feel at least moderately vulnerable to insider threats.

## Effectiveness in Managing Insider Threats
![Chart showing the effectiveness of organizations in monitoring, detecting, and responding to insider threats.]

According to the survey, a majority (63%) believe their organization is extremely effective (24%) or very effective (39%). Another 32% describe their efforts as somewhat effective.

## Confidence vs. Reality: Tool Gaps in Insider Threat Protection
![Chart showing whether organizations feel they have the right tools for protecting sensitive information.]

A majority (52%) reveal they do not have the tools to confidently handle insider threats today. 28% acknowledge having some tools but recognize shortcomings, while 6% report a lack of critical tools.

## Importance of Unified Visibility and Control
![Chart showing the importance of unified visibility and control across the environment.]

93% of respondents consider unified visibility and control across the entire environment—on-premises and in the cloud—moderately to extremely important.

## Levels of Unified Visibility and Control in Insider Threat Management
![Chart showing the level of unified visibility and control currently in place.]

Most commonly, 50% of organizations use partially integrated solutions. Meanwhile, 36% have fully integrated solutions, with a single platform delivering unified visibility and control.

## Integrating Non-IT Data Sources in Insider Threat Programs
![Chart showing the success of incorporating non-IT data sources into insider threat programs.]

Legal data has been successfully incorporated by 55% of qualified respondents. Human Resources (HR) data has been integrated by 45% of respondents, and public data sources have been utilized by 43% of respondents.

## Best Practices for Insider Threat Management
1. **Implement Advanced Monitoring Solutions**: Leverage tools like User and Entity Behavior Analytics (UEBA).
2. **Integrate Non-IT Data Sources**: Incorporate legal, HR, and public data for broader context.
3. **Leverage Automated Threat Detection and Response**: Use AI-driven analytics to streamline response.
4. **Adopt a Zero Trust Framework**: Ensure all users and devices are continuously authenticated.
5. **Enhance Employee Training and Awareness**: Educate employees on recognizing suspicious behavior.
6. **Foster a Security-Conscious Culture**: Prioritize insider threat management at the executive level.
7. **Conduct Regular Security Audits and Assessments**: Identify vulnerabilities and gaps.
8. **Implement Comprehensive Incident Response Plans**: Define specific procedures for insider threats.

## Methodology and Demographics
This 2024 Insider Threat Report is based on a comprehensive online survey of 413 cybersecurity professionals, conducted in August 2024.

- **Career Level**: Includes Specialists (27%), Managers/Supervisors (21%), Directors (13%), Owners/CEOs/Presidents (12%), CTO/CIO/CISO/etc. (11%), VPs (9%), and others (7%).
- **Department**: IT Operations (45%), IT Security (15%), Operations (9%), Product Management (7%), Engineering (5%), Finance (4%), Sales (3%), and others (12%).
- **Company Size**: Ranges from fewer than 250 employees (10%) to over 25,000 (11%).
