## Foreword

In the past year, IT and security leaders faced off with digital sprawl and the profound impact of emerging technologies like generative AI. The growth of attack surfaces has left organizations with more potential exposures from vulnerabilities and misconfigurations rife for exploitation in the evolving threat environment.

Business email compromise (BEC) emerged as a top attack method with 70% of organizations sharing that they were targets of an attempted BEC attack within the last year. Additionally, our data showed most organizations surveyed identified an insider threat within the past 12 months.

Our research also revealed that in the face of growing attacks, IT and security leaders are prioritizing cyber resilience by increasingly implementing risk mitigation and risk transfer activities. Organizations are actively preparing to respond to incidents, with nearly two-thirds of organizations surveyed having a formalized incident response (IR) plan or incident response discretionary fund as part of their security program. And, an incredible 95% of organizations report either currently having or are in the process of obtaining a cyber insurance policy in the next 12 months.

### Methodology
The Arctic Wolf State of Cybersecurity: 2024 Trends Report took the temperature of organizations around the globe and sought to understand how they were responding to these areas of challenge. The survey was conducted among 1,000 IT and security decision makers at director level or above across the U.S., U.K., Canada, ANZ (Australia, New Zealand), DACH (Germany, Austria, Switzerland), the Nordic regions (Norway, Sweden, Denmark, Finland), Benelux (Belgium, the Netherlands, Luxembourg), and South Africa, from organizations with 50+ employees during March 2024.

In this particular study, the chances are 95 in 100 that a survey result does not vary, plus or minus, by more than 3.1 percentage points from the result that would be obtained if interviews had been conducted with all persons in the universe represented by the sample.

## Section One: Threat Trends

### Trend 01: Data Breaches Continue at Alarming Rate

The threat of cyber attacks has long been a major business concern for organizations of all sizes and across all verticals. Our research found that, within the last 12 months, 48% of organizations identified evidence of a successful breach within their environment.

![Chart showing breach identification statistics: 48% Yes, 52% No (broken down into 35% confident no breach, 9% unsure, 8% believe undetected breach occurred)]

As we looked deeper into our results, we found that only 35% of the respondents who did not identify a breach within the past 12 months expressed confidence in their determination that a breach had not occurred, while another 17% of respondents admitted they were unsure if a breach had occurred.

Of the organizations that admitted to positively identifying a breach within the last 12 months, 66% chose to publicly disclose information regarding their breach. Another 30% disclosed information only to those impacted or obligated parties, leaving only 4% who did not disclose at all. In total, 96% of those breached disclosed some aspect of the incident, a vast increase over 2023 where only 26% chose to disclose.

### Trend 02: Business Email Compromise Still a Top Method of Attack

Business email compromise (BEC), or email account takeover (EAT) attacks are on the rise. This survey found that 70% of organizations were the targets of attempted BEC attacks within the last year. Within this group, 21% were able to prevent the attempt(s), 20% suffered at least one successful BEC event as part of a larger compromise, and 29% were the victims of one or more successful isolated BEC occurrences.

Additionally, 61% of organizations identified an insider threat within the past year. This breaks down into 29% where the threat resulted in a security incident, and 32% where the situation was resolved before escalating.

### Trend 03: Ransomware Remains Top Concern

For the third year running, ransomware ranked as the number one concern, with 51% of respondents citing it as their top concern. 45% of organizations admitted to being the victim of a ransomware attack within the last 12 months.

Our results show 86% of ransomware attacks included successful data exfiltration, with another 5% of security teams preventing the attempt. This results in 91% of ransomware events including a data exfiltration component. Interestingly, only 57% of victims were notified of the data exfiltration by the ransomware perpetrators.

### Trend 04: Ransomware Attacks Leave Increasingly Steep Financial and Productivity Impacts

The current median ransom demand is $600,000 USD. Victim organizations paid either some or all of the ransom demand 83% of the time. 94% of those who suffered a ransom event experienced a period of significant downtime and delays in productivity. 50% of victims reported productivity impacts of four months to a year following the attack.

## Section Two: Mitigation Trends

### Trend 05: Multiple Next-Gen Endpoint Solutions in Use at Most Organizations

66% of organizations are currently using one or more next-generation endpoint security tools (EDR, EPP, or XDR). 87% of environments are currently using two or more unique endpoint vendor solutions.

### Trend 06: Endpoint Visibility Gaps Persist as Agent Deployment Falls Short

54% of environments have been unable to reach a 100% deployment rate of the agent to all endpoints. 70% of respondents removed or replaced an endpoint solution within the last 12 months. Deciding factors included:
- 22%: Could not justify cost
- 20%: Lack of required features
- 17%: Failure to detect or alert
- 17%: Value not shown
- 16%: Incompatible with environment
- 8%: Difficult to use

### Trend 07: AI Usage Policies Become Priority

94% of organizations either currently have or plan to implement AI adoption and usage policies within the coming 12 months. 49% currently have policies in place, while 34% have implemented policies which strictly forbid the use of these technologies.

### Trend 08: Effectively Securing Cloud Resources

99% of environments are using some form of public or private cloud. However, only 40% of organizations indicated that they are actively securing all their cloud resources effectively. 44% are trying to secure resources but acknowledge gaps.

## Section Three: Readiness Trends

### Trend 09: Organizations Recognizing the Value of Incident Readiness

64% of organizations have established a formalized incident response (IR) plan or discretionary fund. 64% have invested in an IR retainer, with 70% of those having utilized the retainer within the past 12 months.

### Trend 10: Cyber Insurance Adoption Accelerates

66% of respondents currently have an active cyber insurance policy, and 29% are in the process of obtaining one, totaling 95% planning to have coverage by next year. 53% of respondents were most concerned with rising premiums and stricter requirements for maintaining coverage.

### Trend 11: Apparent Acceptance of the Skills Shortage

Only 16% of respondents stated that the hiring and recruiting of security staff is one of their primary areas of concern, a significant shift from 2023 where 64% cited staffing issues as their top concern. This suggests a pivot toward leveraging external security operations services.

### Trend 12: How Organizations Are Addressing Employee Risk with Security Awareness

88% of organizations currently use some form of security awareness program. 44% develop their own programs, while 44% purchase them. 77% of those with a program include a phishing simulation component.
