## Introduction
Creating successful cyberattacks is a hacker’s job. If they are not good at their jobs, they won’t get paid. This makes them highly motivated to get through defenses and socially engineer their targets. The launch of ChatGPT in November 2022 threw fuel on the fire of the AI race, and since then, the news has been saturated with the ways cybercriminals give each other a head start by selling phishing toolkits on the dark web, how they shift tactics during large-scale commodity attacks, the multiple steps they deploy during an advanced persistent threat campaign, and the different ways they impersonate brands and individuals.

*Unless otherwise cited, all statistics in this report have been generated using data from Egress Defend, an integrated cloud email security (ICES) solution that detects the full spectrum of advanced phishing attacks.*

## The Web of Deceit
The phishing toolkits for sale contain AI-powered attacks and guarantees they’ll get through Microsoft and SEG defenses.

The dark web has commoditized criminal activity, allowing less-skilled attackers to launch sophisticated campaigns. Subscription models range from $30 to $600 per month.

**Phishing toolkit techniques include:**
- Templated attacks (credential harvesting)
- Guarantees of deliverability against Microsoft and SEG providers
- Obfuscation techniques
- Polymorphic payloads
- Gen AI application subscriptions
- OSINT toolkits

**Key Findings:**
- 74.8% of toolkits referenced AI.
- 82.0% mentioned deepfakes.
- 46.0% of toolkits came with money-back and deliverability guarantees.

## Mass-Mail Mayhem
How the rise in commodity phishing attacks is overwhelming security teams.

Commodity attacks are mass-produced, malicious campaigns that mimic spam. While they appear unsophisticated, their effectiveness stems from volume and polymorphic tactics.

**Impact on a 2,000-employee organization over 31 days:**
- 7,382 total phishing emails.
- 238 phishing emails per day.
- 31.75 attacks per hour during a 7-hour workday.

**Technical Breakdown:**
- 51.1% of attacks feature a single graphic as the entire body.
- 82.5% contained only 0-10 characters.
- 86.5% originated from ‘onmicrosoft.com’ domains.
- 72.3% of attacks used hyperlinks as the primary payload.

## Playing the Long Game
The anatomy of advanced persistent threats (APTs) and how cybercriminals failed to breach one organization.

APTs are outcome-driven, highly targeted, and sustained campaigns. Of 84 APTs studied, 52.5% were classified as zero-day attacks.

**Case Study: Real Estate Organization (1,200 employees)**
- **Day 1:** Scouting emails sent to 17 targets.
- **Day 9:** Microsoft and Slack impersonation attacks.
- **Day 21:** Four different phishing campaigns launched.
- **Day 23:** MFA token harvesting attacks (Microsoft, Google, Okta).
- **Day 24:** Large-scale white noise commodity attack.
- **Day 27-35:** Teams-based impersonation of senior executives.
- **Day 40:** Final sextortion attempt.

*The attack failed due to Egress Defend detecting the phishing emails and the organization alerting employees.*

## Guess Who?
We look at the most prolific phishing tactic in 2024: impersonation.

26.0% of phishing emails appeared to be sent from brands without an established business relationship. 16.0% impersonated the recipient's own company, with HR being the most impersonated department.

**Top Impersonated Roles:**
1. CEO
2. Chief Financial Officer
3. Chief People Officer

**Top Impersonated Celebrities:**

## Riding the Wave of a New Age of Phishing Threats
As AI is increasingly commoditized, organizations find themselves at a turning point. Research indicates that while 87% of organizations are vulnerable to AI-powered attacks, only 27% are using AI in their defenses. Organizations using AI-driven defenses are twice as resilient to cyberattacks.
