## Executive Summary
The 2024 Research Report highlights a year of significant contributions to the cybersecurity community by NCC Group. Through pioneering research, innovative tools, and active engagement with industry and academia, NCC Group continues to lead in advancing cybersecurity practices and addressing evolving challenges.

This year, we published:
- **Common Vulnerabilities and Exposures (CVEs)**: Showcasing our commitment to identifying and addressing software and hardware vulnerabilities.
- **Blog Posts**: Offering in-depth insights, technical guidance, and thought leadership across various domains of cybersecurity.
- **Open-Source Tools**: Empowering the global cybersecurity community with resources to enhance security capabilities.
- **Public Reports**: Reinforcing transparency and collaboration by sharing findings from critical assessments and research initiatives.

Our research this year covered a wide range of critical topics. In vulnerability research and exploitation techniques, we focused on identifying and addressing weaknesses in software and hardware systems to mitigate security risks. In network and system security, we developed advanced strategies for detection, mitigation, and protection against potential threats. Our work in malware and ransomware analysis provided valuable tools and methodologies to counter emerging and increasingly sophisticated threats.

In the field of cryptography, we explored innovative solutions for secure communications, particularly in preparing for the challenges posed by a post-quantum world. We also analyzed the integration of AI into cybersecurity, assessing both the risks and opportunities presented by these technologies. Additionally, we addressed vulnerabilities in telecommunications infrastructure, emphasizing the importance of securing critical systems that underpin global communications.

Looking ahead to 2025, we remain committed to the exploration of cybersecurity. Together, with our partners and the global community, we aim to build a safer, more secure digital world.

## 25 Years of Research at NCC Group
_Andy Davis, Global Research Director_

The research we have published over the last 25 years highlights the significant role NCC Group has played in the cybersecurity world due to our coverage of a wide array of topics, deep technical insights, and practical applications.

### Addressing Current and Emerging Threats
We dive into detailed analysis and mitigation strategies for both current and emerging cybersecurity threats. This includes advanced persistent threats (APTs), exploitation of new and existing vulnerabilities in software and hardware, and the increasing concerns around IoT security.

### Enhancing Secure Development Practices
With multiple publications focusing on secure coding practices, secure development lifecycle, and specific programming guidance, our research has been instrumental in elevating the security standards of software development across industries.

### Promoting Cryptographic Security
The exploration of cryptographic algorithms, quantum cryptography, and secure communications protocols is vital for secure digital transactions.

### Forensics and Incident Response
The detailed forensic analyses provided in our publications are essential for understanding how breaches occur and how to effectively respond to them.

### Guidance on Compliance and Best Practices
Our research includes discussions on security compliance, regulations, and best practices which are essential for organisations to not only meet mandatory regulatory requirements but also to adopt industry-leading security practices.

### Educational Impact
By providing in-depth technical knowledge, case studies, and research insights, our publications serve as valuable educational resources for security professionals, researchers, and students.

## Cryptography Services
_Javed Samuel, Global Lead for Cryptography Services_

NCC Group’s Cryptography Services team continued to deliver leading research in 2024. Key themes included:
- **Post-Quantum Cryptography**: Emphasis on standardization of algorithms by NIST.
- **Security Vulnerabilities**: Exploration of cryptographic implementation flaws and side-channel attacks.
- **Blockchain Platform Cryptography**: Focus on the relationship between cryptographic principles and blockchain platforms.

### Public Reports
The team completed public reports for a range of customers, including assessments of `libxmtp` (a Rust implementation of the Extensible Message Transport Protocol), WhatsApp Identity Proof Linked Storage (IPLS), and `keyfork`.

### Presentations
Members of the team presented at various conferences, including Real World Cryptography (RWC) in Toronto and the International Cryptography Module Conference.

## Hardware and Embedded Systems
_Sultan Qasim Khan, Regional Practice Lead_

The team researched a broad range of topics, from wireless communication protocols to confidential computing. Notable highlights include:
- **PowerG Radio Protocol**: Reverse engineering and identifying vulnerabilities in Johnson Controls alarm systems.
- **Sonos Devices**: Over-the-air exploitation of the Wi-Fi stack.
- **Sniffle**: Improvements to NCC Group’s open-source Bluetooth Low Energy sniffer.
- **NanoMIPS ISA**: Development of tooling to reverse engineer binaries.
- **eBPF Verifier**: Public report analyzing the Linux kernel eBPF verifier.
- **Google Cloud Hyperdisk**: Security design analysis of Data Encryption Key (DEK) handling.

_Stefan de Reuver, Lead Security Analyst_

Building on 2023, we improved our analysis and response capabilities for edge devices (VPN gateways, firewalls, and routers), enabling us to technically deep dive these systems.

## Security Research Services
_Jon Renshaw, Security Research Services Director_

2024 highlighted the overlap of cybersecurity with public life. Key themes included:
- **The threat from disinformation**: Research into deepfakes and their impact on civil society.
- **The safety and security of Artificial Intelligence**: Assessing risks of AI models deployed on edge and personal computing devices.
- **Security of Connected Devices**: Research into the impact of the UK PSTI Act and the EU Cyber Resilience Act.
- **Telecommunications and Internet Security**: Research into RPKI, BGP, and 5G Testbeds.
- **Quantum Computing**: Collaboration on the security of quantum computers and their supporting infrastructure.

## Future Research
_Ristin Rivera, Research Program Administrator_

The future of security research is ambitious, focusing on emerging technologies, evolving threats, and more proactive defence mechanisms. Addressing data protection fatigue will require a paradigm shift in how security is delivered—making it simpler, seamless, and less reliant on user action.
