## Introduction
As economic uncertainty continues and the threat landscape grows more complex, enterprises are working to address increasing regulatory mandates while improving their security posture. The 2024 Thales Global Data Threat Report (DTR) offers insights into new technologies, their security implications and the organizational changes for success ahead. The report analyzes global trends in threats to data and the underlying controls, regulations, risks and emerging technologies that need to be addressed. The report reflects insights from nearly 3,000 respondents at the individual contributor, managerial and executive levels from 18 countries across 37 industries and explores their data security experiences, challenges, strategies and outcomes.

## Securing Opportunities Ahead
The 2024 Data Threat Report (DTR) analyzes how core security practices have changed in response to or in anticipation of changing threats. This report also offers perspectives on what organizations can do to leverage data assets to expand opportunities to make their businesses more agile and build trust with their customers.

Market uncertainty, new regulatory requirements, and geopolitical tensions have added further stresses to what has always been a complex endeavor. In July 2023, the U.S. Securities & Exchange Commission announced rules requiring registrants to disclose material security events. In the same month, the EU adopted the EU-US Data Privacy Framework (DPF).

According to the 2024 DTR, 93% of enterprises reported an increase in threats. Malware, phishing, and ransomware were identified as the fastest-growing attacks. Human factors remain a major cause of cloud data breaches, with user error at 31% and failure to apply multi-factor authentication (MFA) to privileged accounts at 17%.

## Key Findings
- **Data Breach Trends**: 49% of organizations reported being breached in their history.
- **Human Factors**: 31% of cloud breaches are caused by human error; 17% by failure to apply MFA.
- **Ransomware**: 28% of organizations experienced an attack, but only 21% have a formal response plan.
- **GenAI**: 22% plan to integrate GenAI into products in the next 12 months; 33% are experimenting.
- **5G/IoT**: 65% cite security of data over 5G networks as a top concern.
- **Compliance**: 70% of enterprises can classify only 50% or less of their sensitive data.
- **DevOps**: 56% cite Secrets Management as the number one DevOps challenge.

## Enterprise Observations
Security and compliance initiatives are converging. In 2024, 84% of respondents whose organizations failed a compliance audit reported having a breach in their history, compared to only 21% of those who passed.

## The Threat Landscape
93% of respondents experienced an increase in attacks. The top threat actors are identified as malicious insiders, human error, and external attackers (hacktivists and nation-state actors).

## Developing Customer Trust – CIAM
Safeguarding external users is essential for building trust. 16% of access to corporate resources is by customers. 62% of respondents cited "security consistency" as the primary challenge in Customer Identity and Access Management (CIAM).

## Developer Journey – Cloud and DevOps
Secrets management (56%) and workforce IAM (52%) are the top challenges in DevOps. 53% of organizations have implemented a formal security champions program.

## Developer Benefits and Concerns – Gen AI
68% of respondents are concerned that rapid changes in AI challenge existing security plans. 50% of organizations are still in the exploration or experimentation phase of their AI journey.

## The User, The Perimeter – Workforce IAM
Workforce IAM is the most pressing current discipline, prioritized by 71% of respondents. Contextual awareness is the most frequently cited challenge. Only 46% of respondents report that more than 40% of employees use MFA for cloud-based applications.

## Ubiquitous Connection, Ubiquitous Threat – IoT/5G
75% of IT security teams are now entering the OT realm to defend against IoT threats. Only 33% of respondents view the carrier's ability to secure 5G networks as a primary concern, suggesting a preference for zero-trust principles over carrier-dependent security.

## Taking the Quantum Leap – Post-Quantum Cryptography
"Harvest now, decrypt later" attacks are the leading concern (68%) regarding quantum computing. 54% of organizations are prototyping PQC algorithms to address future threats.

## Choices and Checks – Sovereignty
Digital sovereignty is becoming a strategic priority. 31% of respondents are driven by the need for "full future software compatibility." Multicloud adoption remains high, with an average of 2.02 cloud providers per enterprise.

## Conclusions and Next Steps
Enterprises should focus on:
1. Aligning targets, spending, and effectiveness.
2. Transitioning from reactive to proactive defenses.
3. Seeking stakeholder buy-in through better developer enablement.
4. Growing resilience through regular ransomware response exercises.

## About This Study
This research was based on a global survey of 2,961 respondents across 18 countries and 37 industries, conducted by S&P Global Market Intelligence and commissioned by Thales. Respondents represent organizations with annual revenues of US$100 million or more.
