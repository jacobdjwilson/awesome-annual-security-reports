## Executive Summary

CyberArk 2024 Identity Security Threat Landscape Report is a global survey of 2,400 security decision-makers across 18 countries that examines how cyberattacks impact identity. This year we find that cyber debt continues to build with GenAI, rise of machine identities, and increasing third- and fourth-party risks.

Nearly half of organizations anticipate a threefold increase in the total number of identities, with machine identities squarely in the driver’s seat (but largely under-secured and over-privileged). This growth in vulnerable identities, boosted by the ongoing AI transformation and pervasive cloud computing, is a here-and-now threat ready to be exploited by bad actors with the AI-powered ability to execute at scale.

In the last 12 months, 93% of organizations suffered two or more identity-related breaches. And, with 94% of our respondents using more than 10 vendors for identity-related cybersecurity initiatives, organizations find themselves tangled in a fishing line of multiple systems, applications, and services across different platforms and locations.

### Key Findings

*   **Identity-Related Breaches**: 94% experienced an identity-related breach at least once; 93% experienced two or more.
*   **New Identities**: Nearly 50% expect identities to grow 3x in the next 12 months. Machine identities are the #1 driver of growth and the riskiest identity types.
*   **New Attack Methods**: 9 out of 10 organizations have been a victim of a successful identity-related breach due to a phishing or vishing attack. 89% were targeted by ransomware at least once.
*   **AI Impact**: 99% have adopted AI-powered tools; 93% expect a variety of negative impacts on cybersecurity due to AI-powered tools.
*   **New Environments**: 84% will use three or more CSPs in the next 12 months; SaaS applications will grow by 89%.

## GenAI: Promise, Potential – And Peril

Nearly all of our 2024 respondents (99%) are leveraging GenAI in their identity-related cybersecurity initiatives. Unfortunately, so are the bad guys. We predict an unparalleled increase in the volume and sophistication of identity-related attacks as skilled and unskilled bad actors leverage GenAI to intensify their assaults.

### Are You Smarter Than a Deepfake?
The truth is, GenAI tools will produce increasingly realistic deepfake videos that will be hard for employees to identify and harder for cybersecurity teams to get in front of. 

![Chart showing confidence levels of C-level executives, cybersecurity practitioners, and US workers in identifying deepfakes]

### CyberArk Insights: What This Means for You
1. **Run It by Legal**: Include legal language in your contracts to review AI capabilities.
2. **Handle (Data) With Care**: Isolate and segregate tools to prevent data tampering or leakage.
3. **Awareness is Everything**: Don’t cut corners on cybersecurity training for service and support teams.

## New Era: Rise of the Machines

In 61% of organizations, the definition of a ‘privileged user’ applies solely to human identities. However, machine identities are the primary driving force behind the exponential growth of the total number of identities.

*   **Riskiest Identities**: 49% of respondents identify machine identities as the riskiest identity type.
*   **Top Concerns**: Security concerns are slowing down automation, and a machine identity-related security incident would require significant manual effort to address.

## Chain Reaction: Third- and Fourth-party Risks

The growing constellation of business relationships can stretch an organization’s reach, expertise, and budget. But every additional “nth” provider you bring into your digital ecosystem exponentially increases your risk.

*   **Supply Chain Attacks**: 80% of organizations experienced an identity-related breach due to a software supply chain attack.
*   **Third-party Identity Theft**: 83% of organizations reported being breached due to third-party identity theft.

---

## Cyber Debt: “Shiny Object” Syndrome and a Blindspot

Digital transformation continues to be the top cause of identity-related attacks. 

*   **Ransomware Reality**: 90% were targeted by ransomware, and 75% paid the ransom but did not recover the data.
*   **Business Impact**: 99% of organizations who were victims of an identity-related breach faced a direct impact to their business in the last 12 months.

### The Path to a Robust Posture
1. **Zero Trust**: Start your Zero Trust journey immediately.
2. **Secure Every Identity**: Leave no identity—human or machine—unmanaged.
3. **Training**: Mandatory cybersecurity awareness training is a must.
4. **Plan for the Worst**: Develop contingency plans and practice tabletop exercises.
5. **Cyber Insurance**: Use insurance requirements as a benchmark for your security posture.

## The Path Forward

Organizations are evolving their cybersecurity strategies with new capabilities and task automation. 

*   **Priorities**: 64% of organizations have or will prioritize Identity Threat Detection & Response (ITDR) capabilities in the next 12 months.
*   **Automation**: Most cybersecurity processes (threat hunting, phishing analysis, alert triage) are currently only partially automated.

## Demographics

The CyberArk 2024 Identity Security Threat Landscape Report was conducted by B2B technology research partners Vanson Bourne amongst 2,400 cybersecurity decision-makers across 18 countries.

*   **Position**: 37% C-level executive, 49% Senior management.
*   **Responsibility**: 81% are the final decision-maker for Identity Security.
*   **Global Reach**: Respondents represent organizations with 500+ employees across the Americas, EMEA, and APJ regions.
