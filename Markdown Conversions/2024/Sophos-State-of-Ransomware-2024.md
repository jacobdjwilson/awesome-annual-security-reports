## Introduction
The fifth Sophos annual study of the real-world ransomware experiences of organizations around the globe explores the full victim journey, from root cause through to severity of attack, financial impact, and recovery time. Fresh new insights combined with learnings from our previous studies reveal the realities facing businesses today as well as how the impact of ransomware has evolved over the last five years.

This year’s report also incorporates brand new areas of study, including exploration of ransom demands vs. ransom payments, together with a heightened focus on the impact an organization’s revenue has on their ransomware outcomes. Plus, for the first time, it shines a light on the role of law enforcement in ransomware remediation.

### A note on reporting dates
To enable easy comparison of data across our annual surveys, we name the report for the year in which the survey was conducted: in this case, 2024. We are mindful that respondents are sharing their experiences over the previous year, so many of the attacks referenced occurred in 2023.

## About the survey
The report is based on the findings of an independent, vendor-agnostic survey commissioned by Sophos of 5,000 IT/cybersecurity leaders across 14 countries in the Americas, EMEA, and Asia Pacific. All respondents represent organizations with between 100 and 5,000 employees. The survey was conducted by research specialist Vanson Bourne between January and February 2024.

![Infographic showing 5,000 respondents, 14 countries, 100-5,000 employee organizations, and 15 industry segments]

## Rate of Ransomware Attacks
59% of organizations were hit by ransomware last year, a small but welcome drop from the 66% reported in both the previous two years. 

### Attacks by Revenue
The propensity to be hit by ransomware generally increases with revenue, with $5B+ organizations reporting the joint highest rate of attack (67%). However, even the smallest organizations (less than $10M revenue) are still regularly targeted, with just under half (47%) hit by ransomware in the last year.

### Attacks by Industry and Country
France reported the highest rate of ransomware attacks in 2024 (74%). Among industries, central/federal government reported the highest attack rate (68%), while state/local government reported the lowest (34%).

## Percentage of Computers Impacted
On average, just under half (49%) of an organization’s computers are impacted by a ransomware attack. 

![Chart showing distribution of devices impacted, ranging from less than 1% to 91-100%]

## Root Causes of Ransomware Attacks
99% of organizations hit by ransomware were able to identify the root cause of the attack, with exploited vulnerabilities the most commonly identified starting point (32%) for the second year running.

![Chart comparing root causes: Exploited vulnerability, Compromised credentials, Malicious email, Phishing, Brute force attack, Download]

## Backup Compromise
94% of organizations hit by ransomware in the past year said that the cybercriminals attempted to compromise their backups during the attack. Across all sectors, 57% of backup compromise attempts were successful.

## Rate of Data Encryption
Seven in ten (70%) ransomware attacks in the last year resulted in data encryption. 

## Data Theft
In 32% of incidents where data was encrypted, data was also stolen – slightly above last year's rate of 30%.

## Data Recovery
98% of organizations that had data encrypted got data back. The two primary ways of recovering data were restoring from backups (68%) and paying the ransom (56%).

## Ransom Demands
Across the 1,701 organizations that had their data encrypted and were able to share the initial ransom demand, the average ask was $4,321,880 (mean) and $2M (median). 63% of ransom demands are for $1M or more.

## Ransom Payments
1,097 respondents whose organization paid the ransom shared the actual sum paid.
- Median payment: $2,000,000 (a 5X increase on the $400,000 reported in 2023)
- Mean payment: $3,960,917 (a 2.6X increase on the $1,542,330 reported in 2023)

## Ransom Demand vs. Ransom Payment
Victims rarely pay the initial sum demanded by the attackers, with only 24% of respondents saying that their payment matched the original request. 44% paid less than the original demand, while 31% paid more.

## Source of Ransom Funding
Funding the ransom is a collaborative effort, with respondents reporting multiple sources of monies in more than four-fifths (82%) of cases. Insurance providers are heavily involved, contributing toward the ransom in 83% of attacks.

## Recovery Costs
Excluding any ransoms paid, in 2024, organizations reported a mean cost to recover from a ransomware attack of $2.73M, an increase of almost $1M from the $1.82M reported in 2023.

## Recovery Time
The time taken to recover from a ransomware attack is getting steadily longer. 34% now take more than a month to recover, up from 24% in 2023.

## Involvement of Law and Order
97% of organizations globally that were hit by ransomware engaged with law enforcement and/or official government bodies. 59% of those that engaged said the process was easy.

## Conclusion
Ransomware remains a major threat. Sophos recommends a focus on:
1. **Prevention**: Patching vulnerabilities and using MFA.
2. **Protection**: Strong endpoint, email, and firewall technologies.
3. **Detection and response**: Neutralizing adversaries before they compromise backups.
4. **Planning and preparation**: Maintaining and practicing an incident response plan.
