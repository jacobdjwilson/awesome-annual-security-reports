## Introduction & Executive Summary

Security, agility, and visibility – how are data leaders prioritizing these initiatives to protect and strengthen their businesses in 2024?

Generative AI has opened up a vast new world of productivity, possibility, and most critically, risk. AI models and use cases are being built, deployed, and restructured within a span of just months. The rate of technological innovation has left many data leaders weighing the balance between substantial gains and frightening setbacks.

In an effort to develop an authentic understanding of the current moment, we surveyed 700+ data platform and security leaders who are asking today’s most pressing questions:

- How should I allocate my tech and human resources to optimize our data security?
- How can I improve my data team reporting structure?
- How are others navigating organizational, technological, and process-level security risks?
- How can my actions as a data leader directly drive better business outcomes?
- What pressing needs and data security priorities should be top of mind for me?

This survey reveals the concerns, responsibilities, and real-time decisions of data professionals who are facing an exciting yet unknown future.

## Key Insights

### 01 AI Brings Great Excitement and Risk
In 2023, AI went from a trending topic to an organizational imperative.
- 88% of data professionals say employees are using AI, but only 50% say their organization’s data security strategy is keeping up.
- 56% cite the risk of sensitive data exposure via an AI prompt as their top concern.
- Just 20% cite integrating AI as a top priority, compared to 35% prioritizing stronger data governance and security.

### 02 Seamless Collaboration Is a Key Component of Data Security
- 80% of respondents store at least half of their data in a cloud-based platform.
- 46% of data professionals say that six to 10 people manage security at their organization, and 33% say 20 or more are involved.

### 03 Despite Data Access Challenges, Data Leaders are Confident About Threat Response
- 44% of respondents say it takes a week or more to gain access to a new data set.
- 94% of data professionals express some degree of confidence in their ability to detect and respond to threats, signaling potential overconfidence.

### 04 Data Security Budgets Are Expected to Continue Climbing
- 80% of data professionals say their data protection capabilities have improved over the last year.
- 77% say their data security budgets have increased.

### 05 Data Governance and Security Is a Bigger Priority Than AI for 2024
Implementing stronger data governance and security controls is the most significant initiative for the next 12 months.

## Methodology
Immuta commissioned independent market research agency UserEvidence to conduct the 2024 State of Data Security Survey. The study surveyed over 700 data leaders and professionals from the US, UK, Canada, and Australia across sectors including financial services, technology, retail, energy, and manufacturing. Nearly half (47%) are senior leaders or executives.

---

## AI Brings Great Excitement and Risk
The rapid evolution of AI and ML has left data leaders scrambling. While 88% of employees are using AI, only 21% of companies that report adopting AI have established policies for its use in the workplace[^1].

[^1]: Source: The state of AI in 2023: Generative AI’s breakout year, McKinsey.

---

## Seamless Collaboration Is a Key Component of Data Security
Data security is shifting left toward data teams. Rather than waiting to discover security issues in production, organizations are pushing security testing earlier. Tools that support cross-functional collaboration are becoming essential to bridge the gap between security, data platforms, and legal teams.

---

## Despite Data Access Challenges, Data Leaders Are Confident About Threat Response
Data access remains a major obstacle, with 33% of respondents citing a lack of visibility into data sharing and usage as their biggest challenge. Despite this, 94% feel confident in their threat response capabilities. Furthermore, 57% of respondents say that data users fall back on "quick fixes" to access data outside of proper governance practices.

---

## Data Security Budgets Are Expected to Continue Climbing
91% of respondents have a documented data strategy, and 77% report budget increases. The most commonly used data platforms include Azure Synapse (26%), Databricks (16%), and Google BigQuery (15%).

---

## Data Governance and Security Is a Bigger Priority Than AI for 2024
Without a strong data architecture and proper protections, it is impossible to safely integrate AI. Organizations are prioritizing hygiene and governance to ensure long-term success.

---

## 2024 and Beyond: No Cons to Investing in Data Security
88% of data leaders believe data security will become an even higher priority in the next 12 months. 

**Recommendations for 2024:**
1. Develop a long-term data strategy.
2. Ensure collaborative systems are in place.
3. Craft a plan to assess and address potential data access issues.
4. Place the right big bets on your data platform.

## Glossary
- **Data access governance / Data access management**: Policies and procedures that facilitate data access, including RBAC and ABAC models.
- **Data mesh**: A modern architecture that decentralizes data ownership to self-contained domains.
- **Generative AI**: AI that uses generative models to create content like text, images, and audio.
- **Large Language Model (LLM)**: A machine learning model trained on massive data sets for natural language processing.
- **Machine Learning (ML)**: A subset of AI that uses data and algorithms to identify patterns and predict outcomes.
- **Shift left**: Introducing security measures and testing early in the development cycle.
